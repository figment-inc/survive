## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "An earthquake hits and the sand liquefies beneath you. Heavy brick doesn't float — it sinks. Whole streets drop into the ground with you still inside."

**Problem:** "An earthquake hits" — nature is the subject. "Heavy brick doesn't float — it sinks" — brick is the subject. "Whole streets drop into the ground" — streets are the subject. "You" only appears as a prepositional object ("beneath you," "with you still inside"). In the most important escalation clip, the viewer is a bystander being described around, not a person doing or suffering as the grammatical subject.

**Fix:** "The ground liquefies beneath your feet. You grab the doorframe — but the whole building is sinking. Heavy brick doesn't float. Your street drops into the ground with you still inside."

This makes "you" the active agent trying and failing. The brick still gets its explanatory beat, but the viewer is doing something first.

---

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The land drops below sea level. You're chest-deep in liquid sand when the harbor pours in and buries you."

**Problem:** "The land drops below sea level" — land is the subject. The first sentence of the catastrophe clip should be something happening to "you," not a geography report. The second sentence is strong — "you're chest-deep" puts the viewer in the disaster.

**Fix:** "You feel the ground drop. You're chest-deep in liquid sand when the harbor pours in and buries you."

Keeps the geography implicit while making "you" the subject of every sentence.
