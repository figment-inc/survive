## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "A coal freighter appears out of the fog and hits you broadside."
**Problem:** The coal freighter is the grammatical subject of both verbs ("appears," "hits"). "You" only appears as an object. The freighter is acting; you are receiving. This is a subtle but real drift — the viewer becomes a passive target rather than an agent experiencing.
**Fix:** "A coal freighter appears out of the fog. You take the hit broadside." — Now "you" is the subject of the impact sentence.

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "A thousand and twelve people died — most trapped in their beds. The ship carried enough lifeboats. Almost none were launched."
**Problem:** Full third-person shift. "People died," "the ship carried," "none were launched" — "you" disappears entirely in the final beat. The viewer is pulled out of the experience right when they should feel the weight of it most personally.
**Fix:** "A thousand and twelve people died — most trapped in their beds. Your ship carried enough lifeboats. Almost none were launched." — Minimal change, keeps "your" in the frame, maintains the gut punch.
