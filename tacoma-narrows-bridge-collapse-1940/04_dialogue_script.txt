## Clip 01 (0:00-0:07) — Hook
NARRATOR: Engineers build a bridge so narrow and flexible that a forty-mile-per-hour wind makes it twist like a ribbon. You would not want to be on the Tacoma Narrows Bridge, 1940.

## Clip 02 (0:07-0:14) — Setup
NARRATOR: You're driving on a deck only thirty-nine feet wide — two lanes. Wind doesn't just push you sideways, it catches the flat edges and lifts one side of the road, then the other.

## Clip 03 (0:14-0:21) — Escalation
NARRATOR: For months you drive a bridge that bounces so badly the car ahead of you vanishes into the dips. They nickname it Galloping Gertie.

## Clip 04 (0:21-0:28) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: On November seventh, the twisting locks into a rhythm. The deck tilts forty-five degrees. You can't stand. Concrete cracks beneath your feet.

## Clip 05 (0:28-0:35) — Ending
NARRATOR: You watch six hundred feet of roadway rip free and drop into Puget Sound. The bridge you were standing on wasn't destroyed by a storm — a moderate breeze tore it apart.
