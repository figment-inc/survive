## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 5/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "The deck is only thirty-nine feet wide — two lanes. Wind doesn't just push it sideways, it catches the flat edges and lifts one side, then the other."
**Problem:** "You" never appears. This is a third-person engineering description. The viewer is watching a documentary about a bridge, not experiencing it. The deck is the subject of every clause.
**Fix:** "You're driving on a deck only thirty-nine feet wide — two lanes. Wind doesn't just push you sideways. It catches the flat edges and lifts one side of the road, then the other."

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "For months the bridge bounces so badly drivers see cars ahead of you vanish into the dips."
**Problem:** "Drivers" is the subject, and the pronoun switch from "drivers" to "you" mid-sentence is grammatically broken. "Drivers see cars ahead of you" — whose "you"? It reads like a splice error.
**Fix:** "For months you drive a bridge that bounces so badly the car ahead of you vanishes into the dips. They nickname it Galloping Gertie."

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "Six hundred feet of roadway rips free and drops into Puget Sound. The bridge didn't fail because of a storm — a moderate breeze tore it apart."
**Problem:** "You" is entirely absent from the ending. The roadway and the bridge are the subjects. The viewer exits the experience in Clip 04 and watches the conclusion from a helicopter. The final beat should keep them on (or just off) the bridge.
**Fix:** "You watch six hundred feet of roadway rip free and drop into Puget Sound. The bridge you were standing on wasn't destroyed by a storm — a moderate breeze tore it apart."
