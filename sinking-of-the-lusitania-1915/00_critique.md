## VERDICT: PUBLISH

This is one of the strongest scripts I've seen come through. The causal chain is immaculate — every human decision is visible, every clip earns the next. The cancelled escort, the secret munitions, the reduced speed: three separate institutional betrayals, each revealed at exactly the right moment. The turn in Clip 03 ("munitions the ship was never supposed to be carrying") genuinely shifts the viewer's understanding of what kind of disaster this is — it goes from wartime tragedy to institutional cover-up in one line.

The closure is gold standard. Leading with the false promise ("too fast to be hit") and then landing on "three-quarter speed — to save coal" is a perfect revelation structure. The irony does all the heavy lifting. No death toll needed. The viewer's brain is still processing the betrayal when the video cuts.

One micro-note, not worth docking a point: "She sinks in eighteen minutes" in the hook — "she" is the only non-"you" subject pronoun in the entire script, and it works perfectly because it's in the hook before "you" enters the story. Clean handoff.

Ship it.
