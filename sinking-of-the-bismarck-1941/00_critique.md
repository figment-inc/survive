## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 5/10

**Problem:** At ~148 words, this is roughly 60 words over the 80–90 target. A narrator reading at broadcast pace (~3 words/second) needs about 50 seconds for this script — well beyond the 35-second format.

**Clip 02 failing line:** "One thousand four hundred men dead. Now every British vessel within a thousand miles has one order — find you and sink you."
**Problem:** Two sentences doing the same emotional work. The "one order" line already implies devastating loss.
**Fix:** Cut to: "You just sank the HMS Hood — Britain's most famous warship — in six minutes. Now every British vessel within a thousand miles has one order: find you and sink you."

**Clip 03 failing line:** "You almost escape. The Bismarck is fast enough to outrun the fleet."
**Problem:** Two sentences where one suffices.
**Fix:** "You're fast enough to outrun the fleet. Then a single torpedo from a biplane hits your rudder and jams it. Your ship can only sail in circles."

**Clip 04 failing line:** "Shells punch through your armor every few seconds. You can't steer, you can't run, you can't fight back."
**Problem:** Both lines convey helplessness. The tricolon is powerful — keep it, cut the shells line.
**Fix:** "By morning, two battleships close in at point-blank range. You can't steer, you can't run, you can't fight back."

**Clip 05 failing line:** "The British picked up only 114 — then left, afraid of submarines."
**Problem:** The submarine detail is interesting but expendable when cutting for time.
**Fix:** "Over two thousand men went into the Atlantic. Only 114 survived. The mightiest warship ever built — stopped by one lucky torpedo to the rudder."

**Trimmed script would land at ~95 words — within workable range.**
