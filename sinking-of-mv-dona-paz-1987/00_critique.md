## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Understanding — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "No lookout is posted. You feel the impact before you see it — an oil tanker in the dark."
**Problem:** The collision is the pivotal event but "an oil tanker in the dark" doesn't explain WHY the collision happened beyond the missing lookout. The oil tanker (MT Vector) was also unlit and carrying petroleum — that detail would close the causal loop and make the fire inevitable in the viewer's mind rather than unlucky.
**Fix:** "No lookout is posted. An oil tanker running without lights crosses your path — you feel the impact before you see it."

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "No one ever confirmed how many died — or how many were never reported missing at all."
**Problem:** "You" disappears entirely in the final clip. "No one" is the subject. The viewer shifts from participant to observer right at the emotional peak. The first sentence of Clip 05 uses "you" well — the second sentence loses it.
**Fix:** "You left no name on any list. Your family may never have reported you missing — and no one ever confirmed how many died."

### Escalation Curve — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "You jump. The ocean is on fire. No distress signal goes out. No rescue ship is coming."
**Problem:** The fire IS devastating, but escalation is linear — overcrowded → crash → fire → death. There's no turn where it gets worse in an unexpected direction. The "no distress signal" detail is the closest thing to a turn but it's buried as one beat among several in Clip 04 instead of landing as its own gut-drop moment.
**Fix:** Split the turn more clearly — make the viewer think jumping is the escape, THEN close it: "You jump — the ocean is on fire. You survive the ship only to burn in the water. No distress signal was ever sent." This creates a hope-crush beat: jumping feels like agency, then the fire below takes it away.

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "an oil tanker in the dark"
**Problem:** The viewer knows WHAT you hit but not enough about WHY. Was the tanker also running dark? Was it carrying volatile cargo? The viewer can reconstruct "overcrowded ferry hit a tanker and caught fire" but the intermediate step — why petroleum was in the water — is implied rather than stated.
**Fix:** "An oil tanker loaded with petroleum crosses your path without lights — fuel spills across the water and ignites." This closes the gap: petroleum cargo → spill → ignition.
