## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "The Stockholm has radar — built to prevent exactly this collision. The officer reads it wrong."
**Problem:** The first two sentences are about the Stockholm and its officer — third person. "You" doesn't appear until the framing line. This is the hook, where the viewer needs to feel they ARE someone immediately. Instead they're watching someone else's mistake from the outside.
**Fix:** "You're aboard the Andrea Doria, 1956. The ship heading toward you has radar — built to prevent exactly this. Its officer reads it wrong." — This puts "you" in sentence one and makes the Stockholm's error something happening TO you.

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "Your crew sees the Stockholm on radar and turns — but the Stockholm turns too, directly into your path."
**Problem:** "Your crew" is doing the acting, not "you." This is borderline — possessive "your" keeps the viewer connected, but the subject performing the action is the crew, not the viewer. It's a 7, not a failure, but it softens immersion.
**Fix:** "You see the Stockholm on radar and turn — but the Stockholm turns too, directly into your path." — Putting "you" as the decision-maker sharpens the gut punch of the converging courses.
