# The Siege of Constantinople — 1453

**Slug**: `siege-of-constantinople-1453`
**Setting**: Constantinople, capital of the Byzantine Empire, during the final Ottoman siege of April-May 1453.
**Hook**: A thousand years of walls. One unlocked door. Everyone inside dies.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of cold stone grey, dusty mortar white, tarnished bronze, deep Ottoman crimson, and pre-dawn indigo blue. Lighting mood is flat torchlit warmth against cold grey stone — the contrast between firelight amber and the dull blue-grey of fortification walls. Atmospheric quality is heavy with dust, mortar powder, and cannon smoke rendered as flat grey-brown haze hanging in layers. Recurring visual motifs: the massive Theodosian double walls in various states of damage appearing in nearly every background, and drifting flat particles of stone dust and mortar powder filling the air in every frame.
