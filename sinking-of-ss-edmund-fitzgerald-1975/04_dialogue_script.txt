## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: You're on the largest ship on the Great Lakes, carrying twenty-six thousand tons of iron ore. Your captain radios that a fence rail is down and two vents are gone — your hull is open to the lake. You would not want to be on Lake Superior, 1975.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: November waves hit thirty-five feet. Your cargo holds are flooding, and wet ore shifts — dragging your bow lower with every wave.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Your bow is riding six feet too low. Every wave pushes more water into your holds. You radio the ship behind you — you say you're holding your own.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Twenty-nine men gone in seconds. No one ever confirmed what broke — because no one required the Fitzgerald to carry a voyage data recorder.
