## VERDICT: PUBLISH

This is a strong script. The causal chain is airtight, the closure is one of the best endings I've seen in this series, and the hook delivers conflict immediately. Minor trims and one visceral beat away from near-perfect.

## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 7/10

**Problem:** 93 words. Three words over budget. Not a crisis, but at 2.6 wps that's an extra second we don't have.

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "Your reactors shut down automatically — exactly as designed."
**Fix:** "Your reactors shut down automatically." — Cutting "exactly as designed" saves 3 words and the viewer infers it from "automatically." Alternatively, trim "massive" from "massive heat" — "the fuel still generates brutal heat" (same count but tighter feel). Pick one trim to land at 90.

### Gut Punch — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "The wave floods the basement. Every generator drowns. The lights go out."
**Problem:** This is efficient and clear, but it's a sequence of external events. The viewer watches the disaster happen to equipment, not to a body. "The lights go out" is the closest to visceral, but it's still observational. One sensory detail — darkness, heat, the sound — would drop the viewer into the room.
**Fix:** "The wave floods the basement. Every generator drowns. You're standing in the dark." — Puts the viewer in their body. The darkness is happening TO them, not around them. Costs zero extra words.
