## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "You have thirty Swiss guards and eighty retired soldiers to stop them."
**Problem:** The numbers tell us you're outnumbered, but the viewer doesn't understand WHY the Bastille — a fortress — has such a skeleton crew. The fact that it's a nearly empty political prison holding only seven inmates, not a real military installation, is the key institutional context that makes the negotiation decision make sense. Without it, the viewer might wonder why you'd negotiate at all from behind fortress walls.
**Fix:** "You have thirty Swiss guards and eighty pensioners inside a fortress holding seven prisoners. You would not want to be at the Bastille, 1789." — This communicates that the Bastille is a hollow symbol, not a real stronghold, which makes the decision to negotiate (and the crowd's fury) more legible.

*Note: This is a minor gap. The script functions without it — the causal chain is clear enough to follow. But adding it would elevate from "the viewer understands what happened" to "the viewer understands why it was always going to happen."*
