## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Your ship has radar — the first generation built to prevent collisions. The officers use it to steer directly toward another vessel. You would not want to be on the Andrea Doria, 1956.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Dense fog off Nantucket. Both your ship and the Stockholm see each other on radar. But each captain assumes the other will pass on the correct side — and neither radios to confirm.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: At eleven miles apart, you're closing head-on. Both ships turn — the same direction. Instead of passing safely, you're now aimed at each other's hulls.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The Stockholm's reinforced ice-breaking bow punches thirty feet into your starboard side. Fuel tanks flood with seawater. The Andrea Doria lists so far that half your lifeboats can't launch.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Forty-six people died — on a ship with enough lifeboats for everyone. Two radars, two crews, and nobody picked up the radio.
