# The Destruction of the Space Shuttle Columbia — 2003

**Slug**: `space-shuttle-columbia-2003`
**Setting**: Space Shuttle Columbia, from launch at Kennedy Space Center on January 16 to breakup over Texas on February 1, 2003.
**Hook**: Engineers begged NASA to photograph the damage. Management said no. Sixteen days later, Columbia broke apart on re-entry.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of deep space black, cold titanium grey, sterile institutional white, and NASA cobalt blue, with accents of thermal orange and plasma red reserved for the catastrophe. Lighting is flat and even throughout — cold fluorescent white for interior scenes, stark flat black for orbital exteriors, shifting to intense flat orange-red during re-entry. Atmosphere is clinical and sterile in early clips, transitioning to superheated haze and debris trails in the final acts. Recurring visual motifs: the distinctive black-and-white thermal tile pattern of the shuttle's underside, and a small wedge-shaped gap in the wing's leading edge that grows more prominent with each clip.
