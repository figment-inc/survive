## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "You would not want to be on Columbia, 2003."
**Problem:** The framing line eats 10 words for a contractual obligation. Can be compressed.
**Fix:** "You wouldn't wanna be on Columbia, 2003." (saves 3 words)

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "the only thing protecting you from 3,000-degree plasma during re-entry"
**Problem:** This subordinate clause is 10 words of explanation that can be halved. The viewer will see re-entry visuals; you don't need both "plasma" and "re-entry."
**Fix:** "the only thing between you and 3,000-degree re-entry plasma." (saves 3 words)

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "But nobody looked, so nobody knows."
**Problem:** Effective line, but the redundancy of "nobody… nobody" costs a word. Consider: "No one looked. No one knows." — same length but punchier cadence, or trim surrounding material instead.

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Managers close the discussion — if the shield is damaged, they reason, there's nothing you can do anyway."
**Problem:** This is 18 words for one beat. The parenthetical "they reason" is narrator editorializing slightly and costs 2 words. The reasoning itself is powerful and should stay, but tighten.
**Fix:** "Managers close the discussion. If the shield is damaged, there's nothing you can do anyway." (saves 3 words)

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "Columbia breaks apart over Texas at twelve thousand miles per hour."
**Problem:** "Twelve thousand miles per hour" is 5 words. The speed detail is vivid but expendable if you need cuts. Alternatively, compress.
**Fix:** "Columbia breaks apart over Texas at Mach 18." (saves 2 words)

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "Management decided the foam wasn't worth worrying about."
**Problem:** "Wasn't worth worrying about" is soft. Tighten to save words and sharpen the blade.
**Fix:** "Management decided the foam wasn't a concern." (saves 2 words)
