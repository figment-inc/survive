## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You watch your engineers flag the test. Managers override them and call it good enough — and you're told to keep drilling."

**Problem:** "You watch" is passive observation, not action. "Managers override" shifts the grammatical subject away from you. "You're told" is passive voice. In the clip where the human decision that kills people is made, "you" is a bystander three times over. This is the weakest clip for immersion precisely where it should be strongest.

**Fix:** "You flag the pressure test as failing. Your managers override you and call it good enough — you keep drilling. Gas rockets up the pipe at thousands of pounds per square inch."

This makes "you" the engineer who gets overruled. The viewer IS the person being ignored. Much more visceral, and the management failure is still crystal clear.
