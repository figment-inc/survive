## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Seine doesn't overflow its banks — it rises through the floor. Paris built its sewers, metro, and basements directly into the river's water table. You would not want to be in Paris, 1910.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Six weeks of rain saturate the ground. Water pushes up through basement drains, cracks in foundations, metro tunnels — flooding the city from below.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You sandbag the riverbank, but the water is already behind you. It's coming up through your own cellar.

## Clip 04 — Turn
NARRATOR: The sewers reverse. Drinking water mixes with waste. Two hundred thousand Parisians are now wading through their own sewage.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Paris spent a century building underground. The river simply followed the pipes back in.
