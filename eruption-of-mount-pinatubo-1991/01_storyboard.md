# The Eruption of Mount Pinatubo — 1991

**Slug**: `eruption-of-mount-pinatubo-1991`
**Setting**: The lowlands and valleys surrounding Mount Pinatubo in Pampanga province, Central Luzon, Philippines, June 1991.
**Hook**: The volcano gave you ten weeks of warning. The maps were drawn to save someone else.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of volcanic ash grey, warm muddy ochre, sulfurous yellow-green, deep jungle green, and military olive drab, with accents of corrugated tin silver and sugarcane gold. Flat even lighting shifts from bright tropical white-blue in early clips to oppressive dark charcoal-grey as the eruption intensifies. Atmospheric quality moves from clear humid tropical air to thick flat grey ash haze choking every frame. Recurring visual motifs: drifting flat grey ash particles suspended in the air across all eruption and aftermath clips, and the stark geometric line of a chain-link military fence dividing protected from unprotected ground.
