## Clip 01 (0:00-0:08) — The Hook
NARRATOR: The volcano gives you ten weeks of warning — maps, seismographs, the full attention of the United States military. None of it saves you. You would not want to be at Mount Pinatubo, 1991.

## Clip 02 (0:08-0:16) — Routine
NARRATOR: You farm sugarcane in the lowlands west of Clark Air Base. The mountain has been quiet for six hundred years.

## Clip 03 (0:16-0:24) — False Safety
NARRATOR: In April, American geologists set up instruments near your fields. You watch them draw hazard maps showing where the lahars will flow.

## Clip 04 (0:24-0:32) — The Detail
NARRATOR: The maps are detailed — every channel, every valley. But they were drawn to protect Clark Air Base. The boundary of the map is the boundary of the base.

## Clip 05 (0:32-0:40) — The Turn
NARRATOR: On June 15th, Pinatubo erupts — the largest eruption in a century. Eighteen thousand American personnel evacuate in good order. You are still in your house.

## Clip 06 (0:40-0:48) — Escalation
NARRATOR: Your barangay sits two kilometres beyond the cartographic edge, in a valley the geologists surveyed but your officials never saw charted.

## Clip 07 (0:48-0:56) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Typhoon Yunya hits the same day. Rain meets ash. The mountainside liquefies. No siren. Nothing.

## Clip 08 (0:56-1:04) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The warning system ends at the base fence. The mud does not. It follows the riverbeds — exactly as the maps predicted.

## Clip 09 (1:04-1:12) — The Human Gesture
NARRATOR: You carry your daughter to the church roof. The mud rises to the windows, warm and reeking of sulfur, and you hold her above it.

## Clip 10 (1:12-1:20) — Aftermath
NARRATOR: Your village vanishes under metres of grey sediment. Every monsoon for a decade, you watch the lahars bury what was already buried.

## Clip 11 (1:20-1:28) — The Number
NARRATOR: The base evacuation is called a triumph. Over eight hundred Filipinos are dead. The maps worked perfectly — for everyone who had them.

## Clip 12 (1:28-1:36) — Callback
NARRATOR: The volcano gave you ten weeks. The geologists drew the maps. They just drew them for someone else.
