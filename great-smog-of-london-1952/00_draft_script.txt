## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: London burns coal to stay warm. One freezing week, the smoke can't rise — the air itself turns poisonous. You would not want to be in London, 1952.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: A layer of warm air settles over the city like a lid. Cold smoke from five million chimneys has nowhere to go.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Visibility drops to one foot. You can't see your own hands. Sulfur dioxide mixes with moisture and becomes acid in your lungs.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Hospitals overflow. You can't call an ambulance — drivers can't find the roads.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Twelve thousand people suffocated in their own city. The government said it was just fog — and took four years to ban the coal.
