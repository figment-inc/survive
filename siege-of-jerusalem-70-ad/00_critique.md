## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The Temple walls come apart stone by stone."
**Problem:** "You" disappears. The Temple is the subject. The viewer becomes a spectator watching architecture collapse rather than a person experiencing the fall. This is the catastrophe clip — the moment the viewer most needs to be inside the disaster, not watching it on a screen.
**Fix:** "You watch the Temple walls come apart stone by stone — and you're too weak to lift a weapon." or simply: "You press yourself against the Temple walls as legionaries pull them apart stone by stone." — keeps "you" as subject, keeps the physical vulnerability present.

---

This is a strong script. The self-sabotage framing is the engine that makes the whole thing work — the viewer expects Rome to be the antagonist and discovers the defenders destroyed themselves. The hook is immediate, the causal chain is airtight, and "You boil sandal straps for broth" is one of the best sensory details I've seen in this series. The one real fix is getting "you" back into the driver's seat of Clip 04. Ship it after that tweak.
