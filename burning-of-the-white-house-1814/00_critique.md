## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Gut Punch — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The Capitol roof caves in. Then the Treasury. Then the Library. Your capital disappears building by building."
**Problem:** This is a list of buildings burning. The viewer watches from a distance — they see landmarks fall but feel nothing in their body. There's no sensory texture beyond "smoke" and "flames," and "your capital disappears building by building" is a narrator summary, not an experience. The viewer is informed, not gutted. Compare: "dinner still warm on the President's table" in Clip 03 — THAT makes you feel something. Clip 04 should hit harder than 03 but actually hits softer.
**Fix:** Ground the viewer physically in the destruction. Example: "The heat pushes you back two blocks. The Capitol roof caves in — sparks land on the Treasury. Your entire government burns in a single night, and you cannot get close enough to save a page of it."

### Narrative Thread — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You hear drums before you see the column. You've already fled."
**Problem:** Clip 02 already ends with the militia breaking and running. Clip 03 opens with you hearing drums and having "already fled" — this retreads the same beat. Two clips spend time on the rout instead of advancing the story. The "dinner still warm" detail is excellent but it's buried in a clip that partially duplicates Clip 02's ending.
**Fix:** Cut the redundant flight and use the space for a turn — something the viewer doesn't expect. Example: "The Redcoats walk into an empty city — dinner still warm on the President's table. A messenger rides to the Navy Yard: burn everything before the British reach it." This adds self-destruction to the enemy's destruction — a compounding failure the viewer didn't see coming.

### Escalation Curve — 7/10

**Failing line (Clips 03–04 transition):** No turn exists between the rout and the burning.
**Problem:** After Bladensburg breaks, the viewer expects the capital to burn. It burns. The escalation is linear and predictable. There's no moment where it gets worse in a way the viewer didn't anticipate — no second failure, no escape route closing, no compounding irony.
**Fix:** The historical record offers a turn: the Americans themselves burned the Navy Yard to deny it to the British. The viewer thinks "the British are burning Washington" — then learns Americans are also burning their own capital. That's the "wait, it gets WORSE?" moment. Weave it into Clip 03 or early 04.

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "The Redcoats walk into an empty city — dinner still warm on the President's table."
**Problem:** "The Redcoats" are the grammatical subject here, and "the President's table" shifts focus to a third person. "You" disappears for the most vivid moment in the script.
**Fix:** "You've left everything behind. The Redcoats find dinner still warm on the President's table." — Keeps "you" as subject of the first sentence, then the discovery is framed as consequence of YOUR flight.
