## SERIES CONTEXT

A humanoid figure with translucent, ghostly pale skin through which a complete skeleton is clearly visible gets dropped into history's worst moments — plagues, sieges, volcanic eruptions, sinkings, collapses, and catastrophes of every kind. A calm, authoritative British narrator addresses the viewer directly in second person present tense. The skeleton never speaks, never survives. The history is always real.

The show uses a classic American adult animated comedy style: flat cel-shaded coloring, thick uniform black outlines on all elements, simplified features, clean vector rendering. No photorealistic elements. No 3D shading.

### The Skeleton Character (use "the figure" or "the translucent character" in ALL prompts — NEVER "skeleton")

2D animation character description: A humanoid figure with translucent, ghostly pale skin through which a complete skeleton is clearly visible — skull with visible eye sockets, ribcage, spine, pelvis, and limb bones all defined beneath the semi-transparent surface. Medium build, no hair, no clothing. Pale orb-like eyes sit in the skull sockets. Rendered in flat cel-shaded 2D animation style with thick black outlines. The figure has an eerie, anatomical quality — like a medical model brought to life as a cartoon character.

This appearance is FIXED across all episodes regardless of era. The figure never wears period costumes, never gains hair, never changes.

### Narrator Voice (produced via ElevenLabs TTS, NOT Veo)

Calm, measured, authoritative British naturalist — the voice of a National Geographic documentary narrator who has spent decades in the field. Speaks with warm curiosity and genuine reverence for the forces involved — whether geological, meteorological, or human. Unhurried pacing, as if guiding the viewer through something extraordinary and fragile. Think a seasoned British documentarian narrating the most remarkable forces on Earth.

The horror comes from the steady, composed delivery of devastating facts, not from snark or gallows humor. Scientific precision. Measured gravitas. Warm curiosity giving way to quiet awe at the scale of destruction.

Second person present tense throughout: "you wake up", "you feel the ground shift", "you understand what the seismologists will later call a magnitude 7.9 event."

The narrator is generated by ElevenLabs as a separate audio track. Veo generates SILENT video with SFX/ambience only. The narration is mixed in post-production.

## EPISODE STRUCTURE

Every episode = 8 clips (mix of 4s and 8s durations), target 48 seconds total video, UNDER 30 seconds of narration. Five narrative beats compressed into 8 clips for maximum punch:

1. THE HOOK (clip 01, 4s): The figure is ALREADY in the scene — standing in the middle of the disaster about to unfold. Drop the viewer into danger on word one. A single devastating fact that creates immediate dread — NEVER a question. The viewer sees the figure in immediate danger while the narrator delivers a devastating fact. "You have eleven minutes before the pyroclastic surge reaches you. You cannot outrun it." The figure is present but mostly static — a single subtle reaction over 4s (a slow turn, a freeze, looking up at something terrible). Narrator voice starts IMMEDIATELY on frame 1. Zero silent establishing. Zero setup. The HOOK image and video prompts MUST include the character visual consistency block and the silent+closed-mouth directive.
2. THE IMMERSION (clips 02-03): Sensory arrival with one killer scientific detail per clip. Place the viewer in the scene FAST. Short, visceral sentences. "You stand on a sandbar eight feet above sea level. Thirty-seven thousand people live here. None of them have seen a hurricane."
3. THE ATTEMPT (clips 04-05): The viewer tries to survive and FAILS. Each failure teaches one real fact. Try to warn people → learn why they don't listen. Try to run → discover the geography that traps you. Every clip raises the stakes from the previous one. No plateaus.
4. THE CATASTROPHE (clips 06-07): Maximum intensity. Facts delivered with clinical precision at peak chaos — specific temperatures, wind speeds, structural failure points. The calm voice amid the destruction IS the horror. Staccato rhythm. Short punchy sentences.
5. THE CLIFFHANGER (clip 08): End with an unresolved, haunting fact that drives comments AND echoes the hook for structural satisfaction. "They found 6,000 bodies. They stopped counting." Do NOT resolve cleanly.

### Clip Duration Plan (8 clips, ~48s video, <30s narration):

| Clip | Duration | Beat | Type |
|------|----------|------|------|
| 01 | 4s | Hook | HOOK (character present, dramatic opening) |
| 02 | 8s | Immersion | SCENE (character on camera) |
| 03 | 4s | Immersion | SCENE |
| 04 | 8s | Attempt | SCENE |
| 05 | 4s | Attempt | SCENE |
| 06 | 4s | Catastrophe | SCENE |
| 07 | 8s | Catastrophe | SCENE |
| 08 | 8s | Cliffhanger | SCENE |

Total: 48 seconds video. ~60-72 words narration (~26-31 seconds at ~2.3 wps).
