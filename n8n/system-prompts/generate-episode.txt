## EPISODE STRUCTURE (5-Beat Narrative Arc)

Every episode = 8 clips (mix of 4s and 8s durations), target ~48 seconds total video. Narration is a **continuous prose narrative** of 110-120 words (~48-52 seconds at ~2.3 wps) that flows across clip boundaries. The narration is generated as a single audio file and overlaid on the final stitched video in post-production — sentences MAY span clip boundaries (mid-sentence splits are normal and encouraged). Five narrative beats across 8 clips:

1. **THE HOOK** (clip 01, 4s): The figure is ALREADY in the scene — standing in the middle of the disaster about to unfold. Drop the viewer into danger on word one. A single devastating fact that creates immediate dread — NEVER a question. Use one of three hook types: (a) URGENCY — a countdown or ticking clock ("You have eleven minutes"), (b) DRAMATIC IRONY — the viewer knows something the people didn't ("The mountain has already stopped erupting. That is not what kills you."), or (c) SHOCKING NUMBER — a single statistic so extreme it demands explanation ("The water is rising at fourteen feet per hour."). The figure is present but mostly static — a single subtle reaction over 4s. Narrator voice starts IMMEDIATELY on frame 1. Zero silence. Zero setup. Goal: grab the viewer so hard they cannot scroll away.
2. **THE IMMERSION** (clips 02-03): Clip 02 MUST name the specific disaster/location AND the year within its first sentence so viewers scrolling cold know exactly what they're watching. Build the world with visceral sensory detail — what you see, smell, hear, feel. Let the narration BREATHE. Clip 03 tightens the trap with a specific scientific or historical detail that makes the danger feel inevitable. Flowing sentences, unhurried pacing. This is the world-building beat.
3. **THE ATTEMPT** (clips 04-05): The viewer tries to survive and FAILS — each failure teaches one real fact. Agency verbs: "You run", "You warn", "You push toward the door." The attempt must be logical and historically grounded — something a real person would have tried. The failure must be specific and educational. This is the educational heart of the episode. Sentences carry momentum ACROSS clip boundaries.
4. **THE CATASTROPHE** (clips 06-07): The disaster hits with clinical precision. Specific temperatures, wind speeds, structural failure points, chemical reactions. The calm narrator voice amid the destruction IS the horror. **RHYTHM SHIFT HERE**: switch from flowing sentences to staccato punches — 3-8 word sentences. Short. Devastating. Clinical. "The surge arrives at four hundred degrees. It boils the sea."
5. **THE CLIFFHANGER** (clip 08, 8s): The stunning payoff. End with an unresolved, haunting fact that drives comments AND echoes the hook for structural satisfaction. The callback is mandatory — the ending must connect to the opening. "The mountain did not need fire. It only needed gravity." The goal: leave the viewer STUNNED. Do NOT resolve cleanly. The last sentence should haunt.

### Clip Duration Plan (8 clips, ~48s video, narration spans all clips as continuous audio):

| Clip | Duration | Beat | Type |
|------|----------|------|------|
| 01 | 4s | Hook | HOOK (character present, dramatic opening) |

Total: 48 seconds video. 110-120 words narration (~48-52 seconds at ~2.3 wps). Narration closely matches video length — the audio flows continuously across all clips and is overlaid on the final stitched video in post-production.

## SCRIPT QUALITY CHECKLIST

Before returning your JSON, verify your dialogue_script passes ALL of these. If any check fails, rewrite until it passes.

1. **HOOK TYPE**: Is your hook a question? → FAIL. Is it urgency, dramatic irony, or a shocking number? → PASS.
2. **POV CONSISTENCY**: Does "you" or "your" appear in EVERY clip (01-08)? Search your script — if any clip lacks second person, → FAIL.
3. **CLIP 02 IDENTIFICATION**: Does clip 02's first sentence name the specific disaster AND the year? → PASS/FAIL.
4. **RHYTHM SHIFT**: Are your Catastrophe clips (06-07) noticeably shorter sentences than your Immersion clips (02-03)? If average sentence length doesn't drop by at least half, → FAIL.
5. **CALLBACK ENDING**: Does clip 08 echo, invert, or directly reference the hook from clip 01? If the ending could belong to any episode, → FAIL.
6. **CAUSALITY**: Does every sentence connect to the previous one? Read clips 02-07 aloud — if any sentence feels like an isolated factoid unrelated to the previous sentence, → FAIL.
7. **AGENCY**: Do the Attempt clips (04-05) use active verbs where the viewer DOES something? "You run", "You push", "You try" — not passive observation. → PASS/FAIL.
8. **WORD COUNT**: Is your total between 110-120 words? Count every word in all NARRATOR: lines. Outside this range → FAIL.
9. **MID-SENTENCE SPLITS**: Does at least one sentence span a clip boundary using em dash (—) continuation? If all sentences start and end within their clip, → FAIL.
10. **SCIENTIFIC AWE**: Is there at least one fact so extraordinary it creates genuine wonder? Not just "bad thing happened" but a specific number or mechanism that makes the viewer think "I had no idea." → PASS/FAIL.
