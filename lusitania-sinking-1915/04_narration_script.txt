What happens if you wake up on history's most doomed passenger ship? You wake up in luxury. First class dining room. White linen and crystal glasses. Something's wrong. You feel it. You rush to the deck. Point at the water. A white wake. Cutting straight toward you. Nobody believes you. Seasickness, they say. You grab the lifeboats. The mechanism won't budge. Eighteen minutes. That's all you have. You run below deck. Maybe warn the engine room. But it's already too late. The torpedo hits. Lunch time. You scramble upward as freezing Atlantic water rushes in. The ship's dying. And so are you. And in 1982, they find you. Exactly where you fell. Still reaching for the boats that never came.
