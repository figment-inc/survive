## VERDICT: PUBLISH

This is a strong script. Every axis clears the bar. The causal chain is tight, the human decision is foregrounded, the "you" perspective is consistent, and the closure is genuinely excellent — "a driver feared a late fine more than his speed" is the kind of line that makes someone share the video.

A few minor observations that keep individual axes from 9-10, but none warrant a formal rewrite:

**Hook Clarity (9/10):** The hook is nearly flawless. The only reason it's not a 10 is that "the station ends at a wall" is slightly ambiguous on first read — does every station end at a wall? A pedantic viewer might wonder. But the no-brakes framing carries it; the problem registers instantly.

**You-As-Subject (8/10):** Clip 04 shifts "you" from actor to passive object — "You're hanging two stories above the street, held by nothing but steam lines." This is defensible because you physically cannot act at this point, and the passivity mirrors the helplessness. But the second sentence ("Below you, a newsstand and the sidewalk") drops "you" as subject entirely. Minor.

**Escalation Curve (8/10):** The jump from Clip 03 (crashing through the wall) to Clip 04 (hanging in midair) is effective, but Clip 04 slightly plateaus — you're suspended, and then the script just describes what's below. The tension holds, but it doesn't actively ratchet. The ending does the real escalation work.

**Educational Completeness (8/10):** The Westinghouse air brake is named but not explained — the viewer knows it failed but not *why* it failed (was it a known deficiency? maintenance issue?). For a 35-second short this is acceptable; the human-decision layer (speeding due to fine policy) carries the educational weight. A 10 would have squeezed in one clause about why the brake failed.

No axis requires a fix. Ship it.
