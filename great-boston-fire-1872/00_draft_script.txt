## Clip 01 (0:00-0:08) — The Hook
NARRATOR: The fire department arrives in minutes. Every engine in the city is ready. Not one of them can be used. You would not want to be in Boston, 1872.

## Clip 02 (0:08-0:16) — Routine
NARRATOR: You work in the dry goods district — sixty acres of granite warehouses packed with wool, leather, and paper. The streets are narrow. The buildings touch.

## Clip 03 (0:16-0:24) — False Safety
NARRATOR: Boston has the best-funded fire department in America. Twenty steam engines, miles of hose, seven hundred trained men. You have nothing to fear.

## Clip 04 (0:24-0:32) — The Detail
NARRATOR: Except the horses. A month before the fire, an equine epidemic sweeps the eastern seaboard. The Great Epizootic. Every draft horse in Boston falls ill.

## Clip 05 (0:32-0:40) — The Turn
NARRATOR: Steam engines weigh four tonnes. Without horses, men drag them through the streets by hand. Response time triples. On November ninth, a gas flame ignites a basement on Summer Street —

## Clip 06 (0:40-0:48) — Escalation
NARRATOR: — and the first engine arrives late. The hydrant coupling does not fit. The hose is the wrong diameter. You watch the fire jump the street.

## Clip 07 (0:48-0:56) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Mansard roofs — fashionable, wooden, tarred — catch like matchheads. Burning timber arcs across intersections. You retreat one block. Then another. Then another.

## Clip 08 (0:56-1:04) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Granite walls hold until the heat cracks them from within. Entire facades shear off and collapse into the street, burying the hose lines beneath them.

## Clip 09 (1:04-1:12) — The Human Gesture
NARRATOR: The chief orders buildings dynamited to create a firebreak. The demolition men arrive. They have no experience. The explosions spread the fire further.

## Clip 10 (1:12-1:20) — Aftermath
NARRATOR: Twelve hours. Sixty-five acres of downtown Boston reduced to chimneys and rubble. You stand in a landscape that no longer has streets.

## Clip 11 (1:20-1:28) — The Number
NARRATOR: Seven hundred and seventy-six buildings gone. The damage, in today's currency, exceeds four billion dollars. The engines were never late because they were broken.

## Clip 12 (1:28-1:36) — Callback
NARRATOR: They were late because the horses were sick. The greatest fire department in America, beaten by a cough.
