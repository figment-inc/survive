## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "In April, American geologists arrive with instruments. They are precise, competent. They draw hazard maps showing where the lahars — the rivers of volcanic mud — will flow when the eruption comes."

**Problem:** "You" is completely absent. The entire clip is about what *they* do. The viewer is watching a documentary about geologists, not living a disaster.

**Fix:** "In April, American geologists set up instruments near your fields. You watch them draw hazard maps — charts showing where the lahars, the rivers of volcanic mud, will flow when the eruption comes."

---

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "On June 15th, Pinatubo erupts — the largest eruption in a century. Eighteen thousand American personnel evacuate in good order. They followed the maps."

**Problem:** "You" is absent again. This is the turn — the moment the viewer's fate diverges from the Americans'. But the viewer isn't in it. They're watching a news report about someone else's successful evacuation.

**Fix:** "On June 15th, Pinatubo erupts. Eighteen thousand American personnel evacuate in good order. They followed the maps. You are still in your house."

---

**Failing line (Clip 07):** "Typhoon Yunya arrives the same day. Rain saturates the ash. The mountainside liquefies. A wall of superheated mud travelling at thirty kilometres an hour enters your valley with no siren, no broadcast, nothing —"

**Problem:** Three consecutive sentences describe geological processes without "you" as subject. The catastrophe is happening to a mountain, not to the viewer. "Enters your valley" puts "you" as a possessive, not as a suffering subject.

**Fix:** "Typhoon Yunya hits. You hear rain on ash. The mountainside liquefies. You feel the ground shake before you see it — superheated mud, thirty kilometres an hour, no siren, no broadcast, nothing."

---

**Failing line (Clip 08):** "— because the warning system was built around the base perimeter. The lahars do not respect perimeters. They follow the riverbeds, exactly as the maps predicted."

**Problem:** "You" does not appear once. This is a policy analysis clip in the middle of a catastrophe. The viewer has been ejected from their own body.

**Fix:** "You have no siren because the warning system ends at the base perimeter. The mud does not. It follows the riverbeds — exactly as the maps predicted."

---

**Failing line (Clip 10):** "Entire villages vanish under metres of grey sediment. The lahars do not stop in 1991. They return every monsoon season for a decade, burying what was already buried."

**Problem:** "You" absent again. Nature documentary narration.

**Fix:** "Your village vanishes under metres of grey sediment. The lahars do not stop. Every monsoon for a decade, you watch them bury what was already buried."

### Rhythm Shift — 5/10

**Failing line (Clip 07):** "A wall of superheated mud travelling at thirty kilometres an hour enters your valley with no siren, no broadcast, nothing —"

**Problem:** This is a 22-word sentence in a catastrophe clip. It flows like an immersion clip. The viewer's nervous system gets no rhythmic signal that everything has changed. Compare gold standard: "Your body consumes itself. Muscle first. Then organ fat." — staccato, physical, punchy.

**Fix:** Break it apart. "The mountainside liquefies. You feel the ground shake. Superheated mud. Thirty kilometres an hour. No siren. No broadcast. Nothing."

---

**Failing line (Clip 08):** "— because the warning system was built around the base perimeter. The lahars do not respect perimeters. They follow the riverbeds, exactly as the maps predicted."

**Problem:** Explanatory, flowing syntax in what should be the peak catastrophe moment. The sentence "because the warning system was built around the base perimeter" is 10 words of bureaucratic explanation during the moment the viewer is being buried alive.

**Fix:** "The warning system ends at the base fence. The mud does not. It follows the riverbeds. Exactly as the maps predicted."
