**ONE THING: The lahar maps. Clark Air Base had them. They showed exactly where the mud would flow. The evacuations followed the maps. But the maps stopped at the edge of the American base. The Filipino villages downstream had no maps, no warning, and no time.**

---

## Clip 01 (0:00-0:08) — The Hook
NARRATOR: The volcano gives you ten weeks of warning. You will have maps, seismographs, and the full attention of the United States military. None of it saves you. You would not want to be at Mount Pinatubo, 1991.

## Clip 02 (0:08-0:16) — Routine
NARRATOR: You farm sugarcane in the lowlands west of Clark Air Base. The mountain has been quiet for six hundred years. Your grandfather's grandfather never heard it rumble.

## Clip 03 (0:16-0:24) — False Safety
NARRATOR: In April, American geologists arrive with instruments. They are precise, competent. They draw hazard maps showing where the lahars — the rivers of volcanic mud — will flow when the eruption comes.

## Clip 04 (0:24-0:32) — The Detail
NARRATOR: The maps are detailed. They mark every drainage channel, every valley. But they were drawn to protect Clark Air Base. The boundary of the map is the boundary of the base.

## Clip 05 (0:32-0:40) — The Turn
NARRATOR: On June 15th, Pinatubo erupts — the largest eruption in a century. Eighteen thousand American personnel evacuate in good order. They followed the maps.

## Clip 06 (0:40-0:48) — Escalation
NARRATOR: You did not have the maps. Your barangay sits two kilometres beyond the cartographic edge, in a river valley the geologists surveyed but your officials never saw charted.

## Clip 07 (0:48-0:56) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Typhoon Yunya arrives the same day. Rain saturates the ash. The mountainside liquefies. A wall of superheated mud travelling at thirty kilometres an hour enters your valley with no siren, no broadcast, nothing —

## Clip 08 (0:56-1:04) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: — because the warning system was built around the base perimeter. The lahars do not respect perimeters. They follow the riverbeds, exactly as the maps predicted.

## Clip 09 (1:04-1:12) — The Human Gesture
NARRATOR: You carry your daughter to the church roof. The mud rises to the windows. It is warm. It smells of sulfur and rain. You hold her above it until your arms give out.

## Clip 10 (1:12-1:20) — Aftermath
NARRATOR: Entire villages vanish under metres of grey sediment. The lahars do not stop in 1991. They return every monsoon season for a decade, burying what was already buried.

## Clip 11 (1:20-1:28) — The Number
NARRATOR: The base evacuation is called a triumph. Over eight hundred Filipinos are dead. More than a million displaced. The maps worked perfectly — for everyone who had them.

## Clip 12 (1:28-1:36) — Callback
NARRATOR: The volcano gave you ten weeks. The geologists drew the maps. They just drew them for someone else.
