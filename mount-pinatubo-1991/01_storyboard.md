# The Eruption of Mount Pinatubo — 1991

**Slug**: `mount-pinatubo-1991`
**Setting**: The lowlands and farming villages west of Clark Air Base, Pampanga province, Luzon, Philippines, April–June 1991.
**Hook**: The volcano gave you ten weeks of warning. The maps were perfect. They just weren't drawn for you.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette: lush tropical green, warm earth brown, volcanic ash grey, sulfurous yellow-ochre, and deep lahar mud brown, with accents of military olive drab and corrugated tin silver. Lighting mood: flat humid tropical overcast that progressively darkens from bright equatorial haze to an apocalyptic charcoal-black sky choked with ash. Atmospheric quality: humid tropical air thickening clip by clip with airborne pumice dust and diagonal sheets of typhoon rain, rendered as flat grey streaks across the frame. Recurring visual motifs: folded topographic hazard maps with red boundary lines, and grey volcanic sediment creeping upward through every scene — covering roads, fields, rooftops — a rising tide of flat ash-grey that slowly erases the green landscape.
