## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Your country strings every emergency telegraph line along one route — the Pan-American Highway. The biggest earthquake in recorded history snaps every one. You would not want to be in Valdivia, 1960.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: You feel the ground drop two metres beneath you — a magnitude 9.5. The ocean pulls back, exposing bare seafloor.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: No telegraph means no one warns you a tsunami is coming. You see fish flopping on the exposed seabed and walk out to collect them.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Two thousand people died and two million lost their homes. The warning system existed — it just ran down one road.
