## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Chile's government builds its emergency telegraph lines along one route — the Pan-American Highway. The biggest earthquake in recorded history snaps every one. You would not want to be in Valdivia, 1960.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: A magnitude 9.5 quake drops the coastline two metres into the sea. The ocean pulls back — exposing the seafloor.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: No telegraph means no tsunami warning. Fishermen walk onto the exposed seabed to collect stranded fish.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The wave comes back at five hundred miles per hour. It reaches ten metres high. You cannot outrun it on foot.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Two million people lost their homes. The warning system existed — it just ran down one road.
