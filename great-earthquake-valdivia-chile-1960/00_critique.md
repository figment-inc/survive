## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The wave comes back at five hundred miles per hour. Ten metres high. You cannot outrun it on foot."
**Problem:** The first two sentences are nature-documentary description — the wave is the subject. "You" only appears in the last sentence as a passive observation. In the catastrophe clip especially, "you" should be the one doing and failing.
**Fix:** "You turn and run. The wave moves at five hundred miles per hour, ten metres high. You make it thirty steps."

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "No telegraph means no one warns you a tsunami is coming."
**Problem:** This explicitly names the tsunami before it arrives. The viewer is told what's coming rather than discovering it through the event. It's not egregious foreshadowing — it's causal explanation — but it deflates the moment when the wave actually hits in Clip 04 because the viewer already knows exactly what to expect.
**Fix:** "No telegraph means no one can reach you. You see fish flopping on the exposed seabed and walk out to collect them." — The viewer who knows tsunami signs feels dread; the viewer who doesn't will be blindsided by Clip 04. Either way, tension is higher.

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "You feel the ground drop two metres beneath you — a magnitude 9.5. The ocean pulls back, exposing bare seafloor."
**Problem:** The script never states that the earthquake displaced the ocean floor and generated a tsunami. It jumps from "ocean pulls back" to "tsunami is coming" in Clip 03 without explaining that the earthquake itself created the wave. A viewer unfamiliar with tsunami mechanics has a gap: why does an earthquake mean a wave is coming? The link is inferable but not stated.
**Fix:** "You feel the ground drop two metres beneath you — a magnitude 9.5. The quake shoves the ocean floor upward, and the sea pulls back from the coast, exposing bare seabed." — Now the viewer understands the earthquake physically created the wave.
