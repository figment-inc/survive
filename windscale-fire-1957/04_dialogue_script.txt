## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: You're running a nuclear reactor with no containment building — just open-air graphite and a steel shell. You would not want to be at Windscale, 1957.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Your reactor's graphite core traps energy that must be released through careful heating cycles. You push the temperature too high, and the graphite ignites.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You watch uranium cartridges rupture around you. Radioactive smoke — carrying plutonium — pours straight up the chimney and into the open air.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Without those filters, contamination would have made northern England uninhabitable — and they almost weren't built.
