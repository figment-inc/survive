## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Britain's nuclear reactor has no containment building — just filters added at the last minute. You would not want to be at Windscale, 1957.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The reactor's graphite core needs regular heating to release trapped energy. This time, operators push the temperature too high.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: The graphite ignites. Uranium cartridges split open. Radioactive smoke pours straight up the chimney — but those last-minute filters catch ninety-five percent of it.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: You flood the core with water. Steam and hydrogen mix. One spark and the whole building explodes.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: The filters scientists almost didn't install saved northern England. Without them, contamination would have made entire cities uninhabitable.
