## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: A clamp is released five seconds too early — while the diving bell is still pressurized to nine atmospheres. You would not want to be on the Byford Dolphin, 1983.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: You're a saturation diver. Your body has spent days adjusting to pressure equal to 300 metres of ocean. A sealed chamber is the only thing keeping you alive.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A tender opens the clamp connecting your bell to the chamber. The seal breaks instantly. Nine atmospheres hit one in a fraction of a second.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Your blood boils. Not from heat — from pressure drop. Every dissolved gas in your body expands at once.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: One man was forced through a gap half his size. The investigation found no malfunction — just one clamp, turned five seconds too soon.
