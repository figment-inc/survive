## Writing Guidelines

- **Continuous prose.** Write ONE flowing narrative, not 5 isolated scripts. The narration is generated as a single audio file. Sentences SHOULD cross clip boundaries — use em dashes (—) to indicate mid-sentence continuity across clips.
- **Voice on frame 1.** Clip 01 starts with the hook — ZERO seconds of silence. NEVER a question.
- **Present tense ONLY.** "You wake up" not "You've arrived."
- **Rhythm varies by beat.** Setup: longer, flowing sentences. Catastrophe: staccato punches, 3-8 words. The rhythm shift IS the pacing.
- **Educational depth.** Each beat includes at least one specific, verifiable fact.
- **Second person throughout**: "you", "your" — the viewer IS the skeleton. "You" must appear in all 5 clips.
- **Sensory specificity**: visceral, concrete details. Not "the fire is hot" but "eighteen hundred degrees."
- **Twist payoff ending**: clip 05 MUST reframe or invert the hook. The viewer should feel visceral relief they weren't there.
- **Historical accuracy**: real dates, real numbers, real consequences. Every fact checkable.
- **Total word count**: 75-85 words across all 5 clips as continuous prose. Per-clip counts are soft targets.
- **The "thank god" test**: the viewer must leave feeling physically grateful they didn't live through this event.
- This script is produced as ONE continuous ElevenLabs audio file, then overlaid on the final stitched video.
