## Writing Guidelines

- **Continuous prose.** Write ONE flowing narrative, not 8 isolated scripts. The narration is generated as a single audio file. Sentences SHOULD cross clip boundaries — use em dashes (—) to indicate mid-sentence continuity across clips.
- **Nature documentary tone.** Calm, measured, authoritative. British naturalist guiding you through the extraordinary, not dark comedy.
- **Voice on frame 1.** Clip 01 starts with a devastating statement — ZERO seconds of silence. NEVER a question.
- **Present tense ONLY.** "You wake up" not "You've arrived."
- **Rhythm varies by beat.** Immersion: longer, flowing sentences. Catastrophe: staccato punches, 3-8 words.
- **Educational depth.** Every beat includes at least one specific, verifiable fact.
- **Second person throughout**: "you", "your" — the viewer IS the skeleton.
- **Agency verbs**: "You run", "You grab", "You shout" — not passive observation.
- **Tension ratchet**: every clip raises the stakes from the previous one. No plateaus.
- **Sensory continuity**: if clip 03 establishes smoke, clip 04 still has smoke. The environment accumulates.
- **Narrative thread**: every sentence connects to the previous one — causality, not just sequence.
- **Sensory specificity**: visceral, concrete details. Not "the fire is hot" but "eighteen hundred degrees."
- **One moment of genuine scientific awe per episode**: a fact that creates wonder.
- **Callback ending**: the cliffhanger echoes or inverts the hook.
- **Open-loop ending**: unresolved fact that drives comments and shares.
- **Historical accuracy**: real dates, real numbers, real consequences. Every fact checkable.
- **Total word count**: 150-180 words across all 8 clips as continuous prose. Per-clip counts are soft targets.
- This script is produced as ONE continuous ElevenLabs audio file, then chunked per-clip in post-production.
