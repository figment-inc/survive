## Writing Guidelines

- **Continuous prose.** Write ONE flowing narrative, not 12 isolated scripts. The narration is generated as a single audio file. Sentences SHOULD cross clip boundaries — use em dashes (—) to indicate mid-sentence continuity across clips.
- **PBS documentary tone.** Open with the routine, not the event. Introduce the turning point as furniture, not drama. Let the viewer discover the irony. The narrator trusts the viewer to feel the weight without being told how to feel.
- **Voice on frame 1.** Clip 01 starts with the paradox hook — ZERO seconds of silence. NEVER a question.
- **Present tense ONLY.** "You wake up" not "You've arrived."
- **Rhythm varies by beat.** Routine + False Safety: longer, flowing sentences. Catastrophe: staccato punches, 3-8 words. The rhythm shift IS the pacing.
- **Educational depth.** Every beat includes at least one specific, verifiable fact.
- **Second person throughout**: "you", "your" — the viewer IS the skeleton. Check EVERY clip — "you" must appear in all 12.
- **Agency verbs**: "You run", "You grab", "You shout" — not passive observation.
- **Tension ratchet**: every clip raises the stakes from the previous one. No plateaus.
- **Sensory continuity**: if clip 02 establishes stone dust, clip 07 still has dust. The environment accumulates.
- **Narrative thread**: every sentence connects to the previous one — causality, not just sequence.
- **Sensory specificity**: visceral, concrete details. Not "the fire is hot" but "eighteen hundred degrees."
- **Callback ending**: clip 12 MUST echo or invert the Hook. This is mandatory.
- **Historical accuracy**: real dates, real numbers, real consequences. Every fact checkable.
- **Total word count**: 190-220 words across all 12 clips as continuous prose. Per-clip counts are soft targets.
- This script is produced as ONE continuous ElevenLabs audio file, then overlaid on the final stitched video.
