## VERDICT: PUBLISH

This is a genuinely strong script. The hook is one of the best structural ironies I've seen — telling the viewer the Titanic's fixes existed and then immediately undercutting them. The causal chain is airtight: mine → captain's beaching decision → speed forces water through portholes → premature lifeboat launch → propellers. The viewer walks away able to explain exactly why people died and which human decisions caused it. The closing line — "still can't outrun an open window" — is earned, not decorative.

No axis falls below 7, so no line-level critique is required. If the writer wants to chase a 9+ average, the two micro-notes:

**Clip 03 (Escalation Curve / Word Count):** At one sentence, this clip carries less weight than the others and the script is 3 words over budget. Consider adding one concrete detail that also trims elsewhere. For example: "Each knot of speed forces more sea through open portholes — water climbs past your ankles in minutes." But this is polish, not repair. Ship it.
