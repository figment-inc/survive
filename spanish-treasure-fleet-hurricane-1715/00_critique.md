## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "A thousand sailors drowned. Every coin Spain needed to fund its empire scattered across the ocean floor in a single night."

**Problem:** Clip 05 has zero "you" as subject. It switches to third-person history-documentary voice — "a thousand sailors" and "Spain." The viewer is ejected from the experience at the most important moment. They stop being a drowning sailor and become a narrator reading a Wikipedia summary.

**Fix:** "You're one of a thousand sailors who drowned that night. Every coin Spain needed to fund its empire — scattered across the ocean floor before dawn."

---

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "You've been told July is safe."

**Problem:** This is mild but it's doing narrative work — it signals to the viewer that July is, in fact, NOT safe. The line exists solely to set up dramatic irony. A truly restrained narrator would present the July departure as a plain logistical fact, and let the hurricane in clip 03 do the work of making it tragic. As written, the viewer already knows the punchline before the hurricane arrives.

**Fix:** "Spain has spent three years collecting this cargo — gold, silver, emeralds. You set sail in July, the start of fair-weather season." — This states the same fact without the "you've been told" construction that implies the information is wrong.
