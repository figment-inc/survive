## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Chicago built a network of freight tunnels under the river and then forgot about them. A construction crew punches through a tunnel wall into the riverbed. You would not want to be in Chicago, 1992.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Beneath downtown, forty miles of abandoned tunnels connect nearly every building in the Loop. They were built in 1900 to move coal and mail. By 1992, nobody even remembers where they all run — but they're still connected to the basements above.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A city contractor driving pilings near the river cracks through a tunnel ceiling. Engineers spot the leak months earlier and estimate a $10,000 repair. The city delays the fix. Now the river is pouring in.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Two hundred fifty million gallons of river water flood the tunnel network. It rises into basements across the entire downtown — the Board of Trade, City Hall, Marshall Field's. You're underground when the lights short out and brown water swallows the floor.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: The Loop lost power for weeks. Damage hit nearly two billion dollars. A $10,000 patch could have stopped it — but nobody thought forgotten tunnels under a river were worth fixing on time.
