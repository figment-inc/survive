## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 4/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "Chicago built a network of freight tunnels under the river and then forgot about them. A construction crew punches through a tunnel wall into the riverbed."
**Problem:** "Chicago" and "a construction crew" are the subjects. "You" only appears in the framing line, which doesn't count as action. The hook reads like a documentary voiceover about a city, not something happening to the viewer.
**Fix:** "You're working in downtown Chicago, 1992. Forty miles of freight tunnels run under your feet — and nobody remembers they're there. A construction crew just punched through a tunnel wall into the riverbed."

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "Beneath downtown, forty miles of abandoned tunnels connect nearly every building in the Loop. They were built in 1900 to move coal and mail. By 1992, nobody even remembers where they all run — but they're still connected to the basements above."
**Problem:** Pure exposition with zero "you" presence. This is a Wikipedia paragraph. The viewer is a spectator, not a participant.
**Fix:** "Your office sits above forty miles of abandoned tunnels. They were built in 1900 to move coal and mail. By 1992, you don't know they exist — but they still connect to your basement."

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "A city contractor driving pilings near the river cracks through a tunnel ceiling. Engineers spot the leak months earlier and estimate a $10,000 repair. The city delays the fix. Now the river is pouring in."
**Problem:** "A city contractor," "Engineers," and "The city" are all third-person subjects. "You" is completely absent from the escalation clip, which is where the viewer should start feeling the walls close in.
**Fix:** "A city contractor driving pilings near the river cracks through a tunnel ceiling. Engineers spotted the crack months ago — estimated a $10,000 fix. You wait for the city to approve the repair. They never do. Now the river is pouring in."

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "Two hundred fifty million gallons of river water flood the tunnel network. It rises into basements across the entire downtown — the Board of Trade, City Hall, Marshall Field's."
**Problem:** The first two sentences of the catastrophe clip are nature-documentary scale shots. "You" finally appears in the third sentence, but by then the most dramatic moment has already been told in third person.
**Fix:** "You feel the floor vibrate before you see the water. Two hundred fifty million gallons of river surge through the tunnel network into basements across downtown. You're in the sub-basement when the lights short out and brown water swallows your ankles."

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "The Loop lost power for weeks. Damage hit nearly two billion dollars."
**Problem:** Aftermath reverts to third person. The viewer is ejected from the experience for the closing.
**Fix:** "You lose power for weeks. The damage across the Loop hits nearly two billion dollars."

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Now the river is pouring in."
**Problem:** Minor issue — "Now" functions as a dramatic beat marker, telling the viewer "here's the bad part." It's not a severe foreshadowing violation, but it's the narrator stepping out of reportage into dramatic staging. In a script this clean, it stands out.
**Fix:** "The river pours in." — Present tense, no editorial "now." The fact speaks for itself.
