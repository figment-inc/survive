## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: You're working in downtown Chicago, 1992. Forty miles of freight tunnels run under your feet, and nobody remembers they're there. You would not want to be in Chicago, 1992.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Your office sits above a network built in 1900 to move coal and mail. By 1992, you don't know the tunnels exist — but they still connect to your basement.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A city contractor driving pilings near the river cracks through a tunnel ceiling. Engineers spotted the breach months ago and estimated a $10,000 repair. You wait for the city to approve the fix. They never do. The river pours in.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: You feel the floor vibrate before you see the water. Two hundred fifty million gallons of river surge through the tunnels into basements across downtown — the Board of Trade, City Hall, Marshall Field's. You're in the sub-basement when the lights short out and brown water swallows your ankles.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: You lose power for weeks. The damage across the Loop hits nearly two billion dollars — because nobody thought forgotten tunnels under a river were worth a $10,000 patch.
