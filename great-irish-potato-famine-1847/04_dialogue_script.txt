## Clip 01 (0:00-0:04) — The Hook
NARRATOR: The potato in your hands is turning to black slime. It is the only crop you have.

## Clip 02 (0:04-0:12) — Immersion
NARRATOR: Skibbereen, County Cork. January, eighteen forty-seven — the worst month of what the Irish will call An Gorta Mór, the Great Hunger. A fungus called Phytophthora infestans has destroyed ninety percent of the potato harvest for the second consecutive year, and three million people depend on that single crop for survival —

## Clip 03 (0:12-0:16) — Immersion
NARRATOR: — and there is nothing else. You boil nettles. You boil grass. You boil your shoe leather, and you call it soup.

## Clip 04 (0:16-0:24) — Attempt
NARRATOR: You walk to the Skibbereen workhouse. It was built for nine hundred. There are already three thousand inside. The gate is shut. You stand in the queue in the rain and you watch a man collapse in the mud ahead of you —

## Clip 05 (0:24-0:28) — Attempt
NARRATOR: — and nobody stops to help him, because while you are starving, cartloads of Irish-grown grain roll past under armed British escort, bound for export to England.

## Clip 06 (0:28-0:32) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: By the roadside, you find two people face-down in the grass. Their mouths are stained green. They were eating the field.

## Clip 07 (0:32-0:40) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: They bury the dead in trenches using hinged coffins with trapdoor bottoms — drop the body, bring the coffin back, fetch the next one. In Skibbereen alone, the burial pits will hold over ten thousand.

## Clip 08 (0:40-0:48) — Cliffhanger
NARRATOR: You board a coffin ship at Cobh harbour. One in three passengers will not survive the crossing. One million will starve. One million more will flee. And the grain ships keep sailing, holds full, past a country eating its fields.
