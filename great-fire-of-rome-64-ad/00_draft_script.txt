## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Rome's streets are six feet wide, lined with wooden apartments stacked seven stories high. A fire starts in the oil shops. You would not want to be in Rome, 64 AD.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: There's no fire brigade that can help you. Rome's vigiles carry buckets and blankets — nothing that stops a wall of flame in a narrow alley.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Fire funnels through the tight streets like a chimney. You run, but every route leads to another burning block.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Six days. Ten of Rome's fourteen districts burn. You shelter in open fields with a quarter million homeless.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Nero blamed the Christians and executed hundreds. But it was Rome's own streets — built too narrow, too tall, too cheap to survive a single spark.
