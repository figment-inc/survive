## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Sensory Immersion — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Unreinforced walls don't flex — they shatter. Your ceiling becomes the thing that kills you."
**Problem:** "Shatter" is mechanical description, not felt in the body. "Your ceiling becomes the thing that kills you" is a logical statement — the viewer understands it intellectually but doesn't feel weight on their chest, dust in their throat, the crack of plaster above them. The script has exactly ONE strong bodily moment ("lungs compressed by rubble" in clip 04). It needs at least two.
**Fix:** "Your ceiling drops in one piece. You feel the crack before you hear it — plaster, then beams, then weight you cannot breathe against."

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The screaming comes from every direction — and then it stops."
**Problem:** Auditory, which is good, but "you" is listening — not feeling. This is observation, not immersion. The viewer needs to feel their own throat raw, their own ribs bending.
**Fix:** "You scream. Dust fills your mouth. The voices around you go quiet, one by one."

---

### Narrator Restraint — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Your ceiling becomes the thing that kills you."
**Problem:** The narrator is explicitly announcing the kill mechanism before the catastrophe clip. This is foreshadowing — the narrator telling us "this is how you die" one clip too early. The catastrophe in clip 04 should deliver the death, not clip 03. The narrator should lay out the physics neutrally and let clip 04 do the killing.
**Fix:** "Unreinforced walls don't flex — they shatter. Your ceiling is brick and mortar. No steel. No give." — Pure material fact. The narrator doesn't know what happens next.

---

### Rhythm Shift — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "Entire neighbourhoods collapse flat. You are pinned in darkness, lungs compressed by rubble. The screaming comes from every direction — and then it stops."
**Problem:** Sentence lengths are 5, 10, and 12 words. The middle sentence is conversational pace, and the last sentence is a flowing compound with a dash. This should be the most staccato moment in the script — every sentence 5–8 words, punching like aftershocks.
**Fix:** "Everything drops. You are pinned. Dust fills your lungs. You scream. Dust fills your mouth. The voices stop."

---

### Word Count — 5/10

**Current count:** ~113 words.
**Problem:** At 35 seconds, this is roughly 30 words over comfortable pacing. Clip 02 is the most bloated — "one million people housed almost entirely in unreinforced brick and stone" is 11 words doing 6 words' work.
**Fix:** Trim clip 02: "Tangshan — one million people in unreinforced brick. No steel frames. No flex. At 3:42 AM, you are asleep." Trim clip 05: "Two hundred and forty thousand dead. Nearly all in bed. Twenty-three seconds. The buildings lasted less." Target: 85 words total.
