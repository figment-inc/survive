# The Great Earthquake of Shaanxi — 1556

**Slug**: `great-earthquake-shaanxi-1556`
**Setting**: The loess plateau of Shaanxi Province, Ming Dynasty China, January 23, 1556.
**Hook**: You carved your home into the earth to survive the winters. In 1556, the earth remembered — and 830,000 people died in the deadliest earthquake in human history.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of ochre yellow, raw sienna, dustite tan, deep loess brown, charcoal black, and muted indigo night sky. Lighting mood is flat and dim — the amber warmth of oil lamps in cave interiors shifting to the cold pre-dawn indigo-grey of catastrophe, then the bleached dust-white and ash-tan of aftermath. Atmospheric quality progresses from still, close, suffocating warmth to choking dust clouds rendered as flat tan-grey layers. Recurring visual motifs: the layered striations of loess cliff faces visible in every exterior shot, and the persistent presence of fine silt particles drifting through the air in flat stipple patterns.
