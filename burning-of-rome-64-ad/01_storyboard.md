# The Burning of Rome — 64 AD

**Slug**: `burning-of-rome-64-ad`
**Setting**: Rome, the capital of the Roman Empire, during the Great Fire of July 64 AD under Emperor Nero.
**Hook**: A fire starts in a shop near the Circus Maximus. It burns for nine days. A million people have nowhere to go.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of burnt sienna, terracotta orange, charcoal black, dry ochre yellow, and deep Roman red, accented with ashen grey and sulfurous amber as the fire progresses. Lighting is flat and oppressive — a dull hazy warmth in early clips shifting to saturated orange-red glare in catastrophe clips, all rendered as flat color fills without gradients. Persistent flat atmospheric haze of grey-brown smoke thickens across the episode. Recurring visual motifs: drifting flat orange ember particles in every fire-stage frame, and the narrow claustrophobic vertical lines of Roman tenement alleys framing the compositions.
