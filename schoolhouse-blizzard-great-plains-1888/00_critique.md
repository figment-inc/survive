## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 5/10

**Problem:** 108 words produces ~41 seconds of narration. This blows the Shorts window. You need to cut approximately 20-25 words without losing the causal chain or the gut punch.

**Fix — cut targets:**

**Clip 01 (currently 33 words):** "You send your children to school in shirt sleeves — it's January, but it feels like spring. The temperature drops forty degrees in minutes. You would not want to be on the Great Plains, 1888."

Cut "it's January, but" — "feels like spring" already tells us it's unseasonable. Tighten: "You send your children to school in shirt sleeves — it feels like spring. The temperature drops forty degrees in minutes. You would not want to be on the Great Plains, 1888." (Saves 3 words.)

**Clip 02 (currently 30 words):** "An Arctic front crashes south without warning. You can't see the building next door. Your children sit in one-room schoolhouses miles away, and you have no way to reach them."

Cut "and you have no way to reach them" — the whiteout + miles away already implies this. Rewrite: "An Arctic front crashes south without warning. You can't see the building next door. Your children sit in one-room schoolhouses, miles from home." (Saves 7 words.)

**Clip 04 (currently 16 words):** "Minus forty. Sixty-mile-per-hour winds. You step outside and lose direction in three steps."

Tighten: "Minus forty. Sixty-mile-per-hour winds. You lose direction in three steps." (Saves 3 words.)

**Clip 05 (currently 19 words):** "You sent them to school in shirt sleeves because it felt like spring. Two hundred and thirty-five of your children never came home."

Tighten: "You sent them out in shirt sleeves because it felt like spring. Two hundred and thirty-five never came home." (Saves 5 words — "of your children" is implicit from the entire script's framing.)

**Net savings: ~18 words → lands at ~90 words.**

### Escalation Curve — 7/10

**Problem:** The escalation is linear — cold gets worse, visibility gets worse, danger gets worse. There's no unexpected turn. The viewer correctly predicts the trajectory from Clip 02 onward.

**Fix:** Clip 03 already contains the seed of a turn — the impossible choice (walk into the storm vs. stay and freeze). Sharpen this as a genuine surprise. Right now both options are presented simultaneously, which reads as a list. Make the viewer think staying inside is the answer, THEN reveal the coal runs out:

"Your teacher keeps your children inside. She feeds the stove. The coal runs out before the storm does."

This creates the turn: the safe choice becomes the trap. The viewer thought they understood the danger (whiteout), then a second failure compounds it (fuel).

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Problem:** The script doesn't explain why no warning existed. "Without warning" is stated but not explained — the viewer might wonder, didn't they have weather forecasts? In 1888, the US Signal Corps (the weather service) had issued a forecast calling for clear skies. That's the human fingerprint.

**Fix (if word budget allows after cuts):** In Clip 02, replace "without warning" with something like "The Signal Corps forecast said clear." This turns bad luck into institutional failure and bumps both Understanding and Educational Completeness.
