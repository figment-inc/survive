## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Narrative Thread — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Your column is pushed back to a ridge."
**Problem:** This is a passive state transition. We went from "ordered forward to attack a massive camp" to "pushed back to a ridge" with no connective tissue. What happened? Did the attack fail on contact? Were you ambushed? The viewer has to guess. The transition works emotionally (things got worse) but narratively it's a gap — *why* were you pushed back?
**Fix:** "You hit the edge of the camp and hundreds of warriors push you back to a ridge." — This makes the overwhelming numbers the visible cause of the retreat, connecting Clip 02's "largest gathering" setup to the tactical collapse.

### Escalation Curve — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You form a skirmish line — the high ground is your only advantage."
**Problem:** This clip reads as a brief tactical note, not a distinct escalation beat. It's more of a transition sentence than a full dramatic turn. The real escalation is the carbine jam in Clip 04, which means the script effectively has a flat spot between the setup and the catastrophe. Clip 03 needs to do more work — either by making the defensive position feel more desperate or by layering in another escalating detail.
**Fix:** "You hit the edge of the camp and warriors pour out by the hundreds. You fall back to a ridge and form a skirmish line. Two hundred men against thousands." — Now Clip 03 carries its own escalation beat: the sheer disparity in numbers becomes viscerally clear before the rifle failure compounds it.

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "Your column is pushed back to a ridge."
**Problem:** The causal chain is: Custer splits command → attacks blind into massive village → outnumbered on contact → pinned on ridge → rifles jam → annihilated. The "outnumbered on contact" link is implied by Clip 02's mention of the village size but never explicitly shown as the reason for the retreat. The viewer can probably infer it, but inference is not the same as understanding. One intermediate step is implied rather than stated, which caps this at 7.
**Fix:** Same as above — make the contact with overwhelming numbers explicit in Clip 03 so every link in the chain is on screen.
