## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "You're inside a collapsing frame of burning metal."
**Problem:** This is technically "you" as subject, but "you're inside" is positional/static — the viewer is placed in the scene but isn't doing or suffering anything with an active verb. Compare "You drag the engine by hand" (action) or "Your skin melts" (suffering) to "You're inside" (location). It's the catastrophe clip — the moment where active suffering hits hardest.
**Fix:** "You hit a hillside at Beauvais. The hydrogen catches. Burning fabric drops onto you through a collapsing metal frame." — Now "you" is the object of active destruction, not a passive bystander described by location.

---

**Note on a minor Educational Completeness question:** The script implies the cut-and-stretch modification caused the fabric to leak, but doesn't state it explicitly. At 89 words you have exactly 1 word of budget. If you wanted surgical precision: in Clip 02, change "The outer fabric above you leaks hydrogen in rain" to "The new outer fabric above you leaks hydrogen in rain" — one word, and now the viewer knows the leak is a consequence of the modification, not a separate problem. Not required, but it would close the last hairline gap in the causal chain.
