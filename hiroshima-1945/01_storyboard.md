# You Wouldn't Wanna Be in Hiroshima — 1945

**Slug**: `hiroshima-1945`
**Setting**: Hiroshima, Japan, the morning of August 6, 1945 — 8:15 AM.
**Hook**: A single bomb erases eighty thousand people before they hear it detonate.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of warm morning gold, soft persimmon orange, and clean sky blue in early clips, violently shifting to searing white, ash grey, and charcoal black during the catastrophe. Flat even lighting throughout — bright and optimistic in the immersion clips, bleached and overexposed-flat-white during the flash, then muted and dust-choked in the aftermath. Atmospheric quality shifts from clear summer morning air to dense particulate haze and drifting grey ash. Recurring visual motifs: the intact white T-shaped Aioi Bridge appearing in early clips and its shattered silhouette in later ones, and small paper cranes or scattered paper debris drifting through frames.
