## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "A wall of yellow mud swallows eleven hundred villages before you can reach high ground."
**Problem:** "You" appears only in a subordinate clause. The main action is performed by the mud, not by you. You are a bystander to the catastrophe sentence rather than the subject suffering it.
**Fix:** "You run for high ground. A wall of yellow mud overtakes you — it swallows eleven hundred villages in hours."

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "You would not want to be on the Yellow River, 1887."
**Problem:** This is the series title drop, which is fine structurally, but "You would not want to be" is the narrator commenting on what's about to happen — it's a soft editorial judgment before the story has earned it. It also burns 13 words on a line that adds zero information.
**Fix:** If this is a mandatory series tag, keep it but recognize it costs you words. If optional, cut it entirely and reclaim those words for Clip 02-03 where the mechanism needs room.

### Escalation Curve — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "Centuries of silt raised the riverbed above the surrounding plain. Your home sits below the waterline, protected only by earthen walls."
**Problem:** This largely restates what the hook already established ("dikes higher than your rooftop," "it buries your village"). The viewer already knows the river is above them. Clip 02 should advance the situation — introduce the vulnerability, the season, the rains — not re-explain the geography.
**Fix:** "Every spring, monsoon rains swell the river. The dikes hold — but they're made of packed earth, and the riverbed rises a little higher each year." This advances the timeline toward the crisis rather than circling back to the hook's premise.
