## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Chinese engineers built the Yellow River's dikes higher than the rooftops below. When they break, the river doesn't flow down a valley — it buries your village. You would not want to be on the Yellow River, 1887.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Centuries of silt raised the riverbed above the surrounding plain. Your home sits below the waterline, protected only by earthen walls.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Weeks of rain saturate the dikes. Officials request repair funds that never arrive.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The south bank collapses. A wall of yellow mud swallows eleven hundred villages before you can reach high ground.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Nine hundred thousand people drowned in a river that had been lifted above them — one unfunded repair at a time.
