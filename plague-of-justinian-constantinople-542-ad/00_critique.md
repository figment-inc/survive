## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "Grain ships from Egypt carry rats into Constantinople's harbor. The rats carry something worse."
**Problem:** Two full sentences with zero "you." The hook is third-person observation — ships carry rats, rats carry disease. The viewer is watching from outside. This is a nature documentary opening, not a "you are here" opening.
**Fix:** "You unload grain from Egypt. Every ship carries rats into your harbor. The rats carry something worse. You would not want to be in Constantinople, 542 AD."

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "You beg to close the harbor. Justinian refuses — a starving capital scares him more than a sick one."
**Problem:** First sentence is good — "you beg" is active. But the second sentence shifts entirely to Justinian as subject. The ending lands on HIS psychology, not YOUR trap. The viewer exits the story watching someone else's decision rather than feeling its consequence on their body.
**Fix:** "You beg to close the harbor. Justinian refuses. A starving capital scares him more than a dead one — so you keep unloading grain, and you keep dying." This keeps "you" as the final subject and makes the trap land on the viewer's body.
