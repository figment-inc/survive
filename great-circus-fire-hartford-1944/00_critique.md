## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "The tent has no fire exits — just narrow animal chutes blocked by steel runways."

**Problem:** "The tent" is the subject of this sentence. "You" only appears in the second sentence of the clip. In a format this short, every sentence where "you" isn't the subject is a sentence where the viewer drifts into documentary-observer mode. The structural information is correct and necessary — it just needs to be framed as something you encounter, not something the tent possesses.

**Fix:** "You look for a fire exit. There are none — just narrow animal chutes blocked by steel runways. Your only way out is the way you came in."

---

This is a genuinely strong script. The hook delivers the premise in one sentence with no riddles. The causal chain is airtight — the viewer understands the waterproofing chemistry, the exit trap, and the melt behavior before the catastrophe hits. The closure lands the dual human failure (coating choice + exit design) with the death toll. The escalation from "small flame" to "liquid fire" to "tent collapses in eight minutes" is clean and brutal. Minor tightening on the you-as-subject axis in clip 02 would bring this close to gold standard.
