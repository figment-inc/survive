## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: New York City, 1888 — every wire and every train runs above ground. You would not want to be here when the worst blizzard in American history hits.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Officials tell you the weather will be fine, so you go to work. By noon, fifty inches of snow are falling, winds snap every telegraph line, and you can't call for help.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You try to force your door open, but snow is piled to your second-story windows and drifts stand twenty feet deep.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Four hundred people died, many frozen in the streets. New York buried its wires and trains underground so this could never happen again.
