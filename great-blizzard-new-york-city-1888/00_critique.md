## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Hook Clarity — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "You would not want to be here when the worst blizzard in American history hits."
**Problem:** This is the show's title turned into a sentence. It burns 16 words telling the viewer how to feel instead of advancing the premise. The actual hook is the infrastructure vulnerability — wires and trains above ground — which IS stated but then undercut by this padding.
**Fix:** "New York City, 1888 — every wire, every train, every supply line runs above ground. Then the worst blizzard in American history hits." — Tighter, drops the "you would not want" filler, and the collision between infrastructure and blizzard IS the hook.

### Understanding — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 02):** "Officials tell you the weather will be fine, so you go to work."
**Problem:** This is the only human decision in the script, and it's solid — but it's just "bad forecast." The script never explains WHY above-ground infrastructure made the death toll catastrophic versus merely inconvenient. The viewer knows wires snapped and trains stopped, but doesn't see the causal link: above-ground wires = no communication = no coordinated rescue; above-ground trains = no evacuation; above-ground coal delivery = no heat. The mechanism connecting "above ground" to "400 dead" has a gap.
**Fix:** Add one sentence making the infrastructure failure → death link explicit. E.g., in Clip 03 or 04: "With no telegraph and no trains, no one can send rescuers or fuel into the city."

### Educational Completeness — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You try to force your door open, but snow is piled to your second-story windows and drifts stand twenty feet deep."
**Problem:** This clip is vivid but purely atmospheric. It tells the viewer the snow is deep but doesn't advance the causal chain. The viewer needs to understand: storm knocks out above-ground infrastructure → city is isolated → no heat, no rescue → people freeze. Clip 03 gives us "trapped" without connecting it to the infrastructure failure from Clip 02 or the fatal consequences in Clip 04.
**Fix:** Replace or compress: "Snow buries the streets to second-story windows. Every above-ground rail line is blocked — no trains move, no coal arrives." This connects the snow depth to the infrastructure premise and sets up the fuel crisis in Clip 04.

### Word Count — 6/10

**Word count: 118 words.** This is 28 words over the 90-word ceiling and 18 over the 100-word hard rewrite threshold. At 2.6 wps this produces a ~45-second video — way outside the Shorts window.
**Problem:** Clips 01, 02, and 04 are all wordy. The hook wastes 16 words on "You would not want to be here when." Clip 02 has two independent clauses that could be compressed. Clip 04 lists four beats when three would land harder.
**Fix:** Target cuts: Drop "You would not want to be here when" from Clip 01 (-10 words). Compress Clip 02: "Officials say the weather is fine. You go to work. By noon, fifty inches of snow are falling and every telegraph line is dead." Trim Clip 04: cut "Coal trucks are buried" and fold fuel into "You burn furniture because no coal can reach you." Aim for 82-88 words total.
