## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: New York City has no underground subway, no buried power lines — everything runs above ground on wires. Then the worst blizzard in American history hits. You would not want to be in New York, 1888.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Fifty inches of snow fall in thirty-six hours. Winds hit eighty miles per hour. The telegraph lines snap — you can't call for help.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Drifts bury first floors. Trains freeze on elevated tracks. Two hundred ships run aground in the harbor.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: You can't open your door. Nobody is coming — every road is impassable, every wire is dead.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Four hundred people died. New York finally buried its trains and wires underground — but only after the city froze above them.
