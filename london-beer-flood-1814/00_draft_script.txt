## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: A twenty-two-foot brewing vat holding 135,000 gallons of beer just burst. The wave is heading for a basement slum. You would not want to be in London, 1814.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The vat's iron hoops have been slipping for months. The brewery patched them instead of replacing them — repairs cost less than a new vat.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: One hoop snaps and the pressure blows out neighboring vats. A quarter million gallons flood into the street in seconds.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: You live below ground. Entire families do — it's the only rent you can afford. The beer pours down the stairwells before you can stand.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Nine people drowned in basement rooms. The brewery called it an act of God — and paid nothing.
