## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Tay Bridge is the longest in the world — and the engineer never tested whether it could handle wind. You would not want to be on the Tay Bridge, 1879.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The columns holding the bridge up are cast iron — brittle as glass under stress. Inspectors find cracks. The railway patches them with cement and paint.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A storm hits with seventy-mile-per-hour gusts. Your train crawls onto the high spans at full speed — no one signals you to stop.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Thirteen spans buckle sideways. The train drops eighty feet into the freezing river below.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: They found the cracks months earlier. They filled them with cement so the inspector wouldn't see.
