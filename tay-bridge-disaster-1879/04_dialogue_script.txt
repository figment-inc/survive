## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: You board a train crossing the longest bridge in the world. The engineer never tested whether it could handle wind. You would not want to be on the Tay Bridge, 1879.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: You're riding on cast iron columns — brittle as glass under stress. Inspectors found cracks months ago, and the railway patched them with cement and paint.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A storm hits with seventy-mile-per-hour gusts. The signalman at the bridge entrance sees your train. He sees the storm. He waves you through.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Six months earlier, the Queen rode this same bridge. The man who built it was knighted for it.
