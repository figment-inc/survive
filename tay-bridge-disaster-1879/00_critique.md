## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "The signalman at the bridge entrance sees your train. He sees the storm. He waves you through."

**Problem:** Three consecutive sentences where the signalman is the grammatical subject acting. "You" disappears entirely from the clip — the viewer becomes a passive observer watching someone else make the fatal call. The drama is right, but the POV drifts to third person at the script's most critical decision point.

**Fix:** "You slow at the bridge entrance. The signalman sees the storm. He waves you through." — Restores "you" as subject in the first sentence, keeps the signalman's fatal agency, and maintains the gut-punch rhythm of the three-beat structure. Also shaves one word.

### Word Count — 7/10

**Problem:** At 95 words, the script runs ~36.5 seconds at 2.6 wps. Not a dealbreaker but sits above the sweet spot. The fix above trims one word. One more pass could get this to 90.

**Possible trims:**
- Clip 02: "brittle as glass under stress" → "brittle as glass" (saves 2 words, "under stress" is implied by the context)
- Clip 03: "at the bridge entrance" → "at the entrance" (saves 1 word)

These three cuts bring it to ~91 words — right at the edge of the window.

This is a strong script. The closure is genuinely exceptional — one of the best ending beats I've seen in this format. The Queen detail doesn't summarize, it detonates. The causal chain is complete, the human failures are visible, and the "you" perspective mostly holds. Fix the Clip 03 POV drift, trim 4-5 words, and ship it.
