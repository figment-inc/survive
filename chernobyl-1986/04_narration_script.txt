What happens if you're working the night shift at Chernobyl thirty seconds before Reactor Four explodes?

You are standing at the controls of an RBMK reactor. You are running a safety test. The power has dropped to two hundred megawatts. Someone behind you says keep going.

You press the emergency button. AZ-5. The one that is supposed to shut everything down. The control rods jam. The power does not drop. It spikes. Thirty thousand megawatts. One hundred times what this reactor was built for.

Two explosions. The thousand-ton reactor lid launches into the air like a coin. The building tears open above you. You are on the floor. You cannot hear anything.

You stagger outside. The reactor core is open. It is glowing blue. There are graphite blocks on the ground. You pick one up. It burns your hand. You do not know it yet. But you have just received a lethal dose of radiation.

A supervisor arrives. He says the reactor is intact. The dosimeter reads three point six roentgen. Not great, not terrible. But the dosimeter only goes to three point six. The actual level is fifteen thousand.

They send you to the roof. Clear the graphite. You get ninety seconds. The radiation up here is twelve thousand roentgen per hour. A lethal dose takes forty-five seconds. You are on the roof for ninety.

From the roof you can see directly into the reactor. The core is burning at two thousand degrees. The air tastes like metal. Your skin is already turning red. You have minutes before the vomiting starts.

Three kilometers away, Pripyat. Forty-nine thousand people. Children are playing outside. No one has been told. The evacuation will not come for thirty-six hours.

The helicopters come. They drop sand and boron into the open core. The pilots fly directly through the plume. They will be dead within weeks. The sand does almost nothing.

Six hundred thousand men. They call them liquidators. Sent to clean a disaster that cannot be cleaned. The robots they send in break down from radiation. So they send men instead. The men call themselves bio-robots.

They build a sarcophagus. Concrete and steel. They say it will last thirty years. It lasted twenty-nine. The thing inside will be dangerous for twenty thousand.

The forest has grown back. The wolves have returned. The radiation will outlast everything you have ever known. You will not be remembered for even one of those twenty thousand years.
