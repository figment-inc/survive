## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 3/10

**Problem:** At 178 words and ~2.6 wps, this runs approximately 68 seconds — 13 seconds over the Shorts ceiling. Every clip is slightly overwritten. The story is strong enough to survive trimming.

**Failing lines (Clip 01):** "Tonight, a single ship loses three times that many — and almost nobody remembers."
**Problem:** "Tonight" is a nice framing device but costs words. "Almost nobody remembers" duplicates what Clip 05 does better.
**Fix:** "The Titanic killed 1,500 people. One ship lost three times that many. You would not want to be on the Wilhelm Gustloff, 1945." (saves ~8 words)

**Failing lines (Clip 02):** "Millions of German civilians are fleeing west, and the only way out is across the frozen Baltic."
**Problem:** Two clauses that can compress.
**Fix:** "Millions of German civilians are fleeing west across the frozen Baltic." (saves ~6 words)

**Failing lines (Clip 03):** "The captain turns on navigation lights because he's afraid of a collision with another refugee ship — which means a Soviet submarine can see you from miles away."
**Problem:** This is the best explanatory sentence in the script but it's 28 words. Can tighten.
**Fix:** "The captain switches on navigation lights — afraid of hitting another refugee ship. A Soviet submarine can now see you from miles away." (saves ~3 words, also restores "you" as subject of the consequence)

**Failing lines (Clip 05):** "It was the deadliest shipwreck in history, and the world barely noticed, because in January 1945, everyone was dying."
**Problem:** Slightly verbose for a closer.
**Fix:** "The deadliest shipwreck in history — and the world barely noticed. In January 1945, everyone was dying." (saves ~3 words, hits harder)

**Combined effect:** These trims bring the script to roughly 135–138 words — inside the window.
