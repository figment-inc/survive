## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Titanic killed 1,500 people. Tonight, a single ship loses three times that many — and almost nobody remembers. You would not want to be on the Wilhelm Gustloff, 1945.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The Soviet army is sweeping across East Prussia. Millions of German civilians are fleeing west, and the only way out is across the frozen Baltic. You pack onto a cruise liner built for 1,500 passengers — alongside ten thousand other people.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: The ship has no escort. Its anti-aircraft guns are frozen solid. The captain turns on navigation lights because he's afraid of a collision with another refugee ship — which means a Soviet submarine can see you from miles away.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Three torpedoes hit just after nine PM. The ship lists so fast that lifeboats on one side can't launch. You're in the Baltic in January — the water is below freezing. You have minutes.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Over nine thousand people died — most of them women and children running from the war. It was the deadliest shipwreck in history, and the world barely noticed, because in January 1945, everyone was dying.
