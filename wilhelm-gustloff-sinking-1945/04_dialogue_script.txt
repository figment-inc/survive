## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Titanic killed 1,500 people. One ship lost three times that many. You would not want to be on the Wilhelm Gustloff, 1945.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The Soviet army is sweeping across East Prussia. Millions of German civilians are fleeing west across the frozen Baltic, and you pack onto a cruise liner built for 1,500 passengers — alongside ten thousand other people.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You're on a ship with no escort. The anti-aircraft guns are frozen solid. The captain switches on navigation lights — afraid of hitting another refugee ship. A Soviet submarine can now see you from miles away.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Three torpedoes hit just after nine PM. The ship lists so fast that lifeboats on one side can't launch. You're in the Baltic in January — the water is below freezing. You have minutes.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Over nine thousand people died, most of them women and children running from the war. The deadliest shipwreck in history — and the world barely noticed. In January 1945, everyone was dying.
