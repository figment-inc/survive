## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "The fourth-floor walkway tears free and crashes into the second. Both slam into the crowd below."

**Problem:** "You" appears only as a listener in the first sentence ("You hear steel scream"), then disappears entirely. The walkways become the grammatical subjects doing all the action — tearing, crashing, slamming. This is the catastrophe clip, where the rubric demands "you" acts or suffers, not watches. The viewer becomes a camera instead of a victim.

**Fix:** "You hear steel scream overhead. The fourth-floor walkway tears free and slams you into the ground — two walkways, two thousand tons of concrete, straight down into the crowd."

This keeps "you" as the object of direct physical violence rather than a bystander narrating what structures do to other structures. Even making "you" the one being hit maintains the second-person embodiment better than letting the walkways act on "the crowd below" — an abstraction that distances the viewer at the worst possible moment.
