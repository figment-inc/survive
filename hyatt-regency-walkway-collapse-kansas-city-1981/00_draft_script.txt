## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Two thousand people are dancing in the Hyatt Regency lobby. Above them, two concrete walkways are hanging from steel rods that were built wrong. You would not want to be in Kansas City, 1981.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The original design called for one continuous rod through both walkways. Someone changed it to two shorter rods — doubling the load on a single connection.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Nobody catches the change. No engineer rechecks the math. The building passes inspection.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The fourth-floor walkway tears free and crashes into the second. Both slam into the crowd below.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: 114 people killed by a design change no one reviewed. The deadliest structural collapse in American history — because one bolt carried twice what it was meant to hold.
