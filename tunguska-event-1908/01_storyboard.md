# The Tunguska Event — 1908

**Slug**: `tunguska-event-1908`
**Setting**: The remote Podkamennaya Tunguska River basin, Central Siberia, early morning of June 30, 1908.
**Hook**: Something just leveled eight hundred square miles of forest. No one knows what it was.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of deep boreal green, charcoal grey, cold birch-white, and searing incandescent orange-white for the blast. Flat even lighting shifts from cool pre-dawn blue-grey in early clips to blinding white-orange during the catastrophe, then settles into ashen grey-green for the aftermath. Atmospheric quality defined by clean Siberian air giving way to flat grey smoke haze and drifting charcoal particulate. Recurring visual motifs: endless birch and larch forest stretching to every horizon, and radial patterns of flattened timber fanning outward from a central point.
