## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Narrator Restraint — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "You would not want to be inside on December 30th, 1903."

**Problem:** This is the narrator explicitly foreshadowing doom. It tells the viewer "something terrible is about to happen" before a single fact has been established. It's the show's title formula, yes — but deployed here it functions as a neon sign saying "disaster incoming," which deflates the tension that Clips 02-03 are supposed to build. The viewer already knows the building fails before you've told them anything about the building. It also burns 14 words on a line that conveys zero mechanism.

**Fix:** Fold the date into the setup and let the passed inspections do the ominous work on their own. Replace with something that deepens the human decision:

*"Chicago's newest theatre just passed every safety inspection the city requires. Six exit doors are bolted shut — to keep people from sneaking in."*

This keeps the hook's irony (passed inspections) but now immediately introduces the human failure (bolted exits) without the narrator waving a flag. The "you wouldn't wanna be" energy comes from the facts, not from being told. Also recovers words you can spend elsewhere or trim the total back to ~85.
