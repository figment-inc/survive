## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Iroquois Theatre advertised itself as fireproof. It has no fire escapes, no sprinklers, and exit doors that only open inward. You would not want to be in Chicago, 1903.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Nineteen hundred seats, every one full — a Wednesday matinee packed with women and children.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: A stage light ignites a curtain. The asbestos safety curtain jams halfway down, funneling a backdraft straight into the audience.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: You reach an exit. It opens inward. The crowd behind you pins it shut. Fire escapes outside were never finished — doors open onto bare ledges thirty feet up.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: 602 people died in fifteen minutes inside a fireproof building — because every safety feature was decoration.
