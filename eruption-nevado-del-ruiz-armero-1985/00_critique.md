### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Line (Clip 04):** "Three meters of mud bury you and your entire town in minutes."
**Problem:** "You" is the grammatical object here — the mud buries you. In the catastrophe clip, where agency matters most, you're purely a recipient. The ground-shaking sentence is better ("You feel the ground shake") but it's sensory, not active. Ideally in the catastrophe clip, "you" is doing something — trying and failing — not just being hit.
**Fix:** "You run for the river bridge. Three meters of mud swallows you, the bridge, and twenty-three thousand people in minutes." — This gives you an action verb before the destruction lands, and moves the death toll into the moment of impact (freeing the ending to focus purely on the government failure).
