## Clip 01 (0:00-0:07) — Hook
NARRATOR: The ocean pulls back hundreds of meters from the beach. You walk out onto the exposed seafloor. You would not want to be on the coast of the Indian Ocean, 2004.

## Clip 02 (0:07-0:14) — Setup
NARRATOR: A magnitude 9.1 earthquake just ruptured the seafloor, displacing a wall of water moving toward you at the speed of a jet. The Pacific has tsunami sirens. The Indian Ocean has none.

## Clip 03 (0:14-0:21) — Escalation
NARRATOR: You have no warning system. No sirens. The wave has been crossing open ocean for hours — and you're still standing on the beach.

## Clip 04 (0:21-0:28) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The water doesn't crash and retreat. It rises. Three stories high, carrying cars, boats, buildings. You run.

## Clip 05 (0:28-0:35) — Ending
NARRATOR: Two hundred and thirty thousand people died across fourteen countries. You watched the ocean pull back — you just didn't know what it meant.
