## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "you watch one sick person become a hundred"
**Problem:** "You watch" is passive observation, not action or suffering. In the escalation clip where the disaster is accelerating, "you" should be experiencing or enduring, not spectating. The first half of this clip ("The sickness starts in your eyes and throat, then tears through every organ") is excellent — you ARE the sick person. Then the camera pulls out to you watching others, which breaks the embodiment.
**Fix:** "The sickness starts in your eyes and throat, then tears through every organ. Packed this tight, one sick person becomes a hundred — including you."

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "you spend everything, expecting to die tomorrow"
**Problem:** "Expecting to die tomorrow" is the narrator interpreting your mental state and telegraphing the outcome. It's mild — not a "little did you know" — but it edges into commentary on what's coming rather than letting the action speak. The behavior itself (spending everything) already implies the expectation. Stating it is redundant narration.
**Fix:** "Laws mean nothing — you spend everything you have in a single night." The behavior alone communicates the hopelessness without the narrator explaining your psychology.
