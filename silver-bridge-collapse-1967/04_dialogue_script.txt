## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: For forty years, no one has checked the steel links below the waterline. You would not want to be on the Silver Bridge, 1967.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: You're driving across a bridge held up by eyebar chains — each link is one solid piece of steel. Most bridges use dozens of redundant wires. This one has no backup.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You're sitting in rush-hour traffic. Beneath you, one link has a crack two millimetres deep. The weight of every car on this deck splits it open.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: Forty-six people died — because one chain had no second strand, and no one ever checked.
