## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: The Silver Bridge has never been inspected below the waterline. Forty years of rust are hiding in a single steel link. You would not want to be on the Silver Bridge, 1967.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Most bridges use redundant cables — dozens of wires, so if one fails, the others hold. The Silver Bridge uses eyebar chains — each link is one solid piece with no backup.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: One link has a crack two millimetres deep. Rush-hour traffic loads the deck. The crack splits.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: One link fails, the next takes double the load and snaps. The entire bridge peels apart in under a minute. Cars drop into the Ohio River.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: Forty-six people died — because one chain had no second strand, and no one ever checked.
