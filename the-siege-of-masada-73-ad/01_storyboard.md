# The Siege of Masada — 73 AD

**Slug**: `the-siege-of-masada-73-ad`
**Setting**: The mountain fortress of Masada overlooking the Dead Sea, Judean Desert, 73 AD.
**Hook**: Rome sent eight thousand soldiers to kill nine hundred people on a rock. When they finally broke through, no one was left to kill.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of bleached sandstone tan, burnt sienna, Dead Sea turquoise-grey, dusty ochre, and dry bone white, with accents of Roman iron-black and ember orange. Harsh flat desert lighting — bright overhead sun producing short, stark flat shadows on pale terrain. Dry, cloudless atmosphere with fine pale dust hanging in the air, occasional heat-shimmer suggested by wavy outline distortion on the horizon. Recurring visual motifs: the immense cliff edge dropping into haze, and the slowly growing Roman siege ramp — a pale scar of packed earth creeping upward against the mountain in every wide shot.
