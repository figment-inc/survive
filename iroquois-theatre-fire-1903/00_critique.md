## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Word Count — 5/10

**Problem:** At ~116 words, this script runs 26 words over the 90-word ceiling. That's roughly 10 extra seconds of narration — a full extra clip's worth. The quality is strong enough that this is a surgery problem, not a rewrite problem. You need to cut ~30 words without losing causal links.

**Clip 01 failing line:** "A brand-new Chicago theater advertises itself as 'absolutely fireproof.' It has no sprinklers, no fire alarms, and exit doors that only open inward."
**Problem:** The three-item list (sprinklers, alarms, inward doors) is effective but expensive at 20 words. The inward doors are the payoff in Clip 04 — stating them here spends words AND diminishes the later beat.
**Fix:** "A brand-new Chicago theater advertises itself as 'absolutely fireproof.' It has no sprinklers and no fire alarms. You would not want to be at the Iroquois Theatre, 1903."
(Cut inward doors here — let them land fresh in Clip 04. Saves ~7 words.)

**Clip 02 failing line:** "You're one of two thousand people — mostly women and children — packed into a holiday matinee. A spotlight ignites a muslin curtain backstage, and the fire begins to climb."
**Problem:** "and the fire begins to climb" is atmospheric but costs 6 words. The ignition IS the fire beginning.
**Fix:** "You're one of two thousand people — mostly women and children — packed into a holiday matinee. A spotlight ignites a muslin curtain backstage."

**Clip 03 failing line:** "The asbestos safety curtain descends, but it snags halfway down on a wire. You watch open flame curl beneath it."
**Problem:** "You watch open flame curl beneath it" is gorgeous but expensive at 8 words when the backdraft is about to make it irrelevant.
**Fix:** "The asbestos safety curtain descends — and snags halfway on a wire. A stagehand opens the rear loading door."
(Merge the stagehand beat into Clip 03 to tighten the transition.)

**Clip 05 failing line:** "Every exit was locked or bolted to keep people from sneaking in without tickets."
**Problem:** "locked or bolted" is redundant — pick one.
**Fix:** "Every exit was bolted shut — to keep people from sneaking in without tickets."
