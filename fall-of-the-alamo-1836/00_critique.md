## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### Narrator Restraint — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You see Santa Anna raise a red flag — no quarter, no surrender accepted."

**Problem:** This is borderline. The line is factual and grounded — it's something "you" literally observe. But the explanatory gloss "no quarter, no surrender accepted" functions as the narrator stepping in to decode the symbol AND signal the coming slaughter. It's mild, but it nudges the audience toward "everyone is about to die" before Clip 04 delivers it. The red flag detail is excellent; the narrator just over-explains its meaning.

**Fix:** "You see Santa Anna raise a red flag over his camp." — Let the audience sit with that image. The catastrophe in Clip 04 delivers the meaning. If the audience doesn't know what the red flag means, that's fine — they'll understand it retroactively in three seconds. The not-knowing actually increases tension.

This is a strong script. The hook is one of the cleanest I've seen — three facts, each one worse than the last, zero confusion. The human decision (Houston refusing reinforcements) is planted in Clip 02 and paid off in the closer, which gives the whole piece a structural backbone most Alamo retellings lack. Travis's line in the dirt is a perfect "you-as-subject" moment — you make a choice, and the rest of the script is the consequence of that choice.

The catastrophe clip is excellent: "You fire once, maybe twice. Then they're over the top." Staccato, physical, final. No grandstanding.

Ship it.
