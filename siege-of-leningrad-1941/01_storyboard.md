# The Siege of Leningrad — 1941

**Slug**: `siege-of-leningrad-1941`
**Setting**: Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg), Soviet Union, during the 872-day Nazi German blockade beginning September 1941.
**Hook**: The bombs didn't kill Leningrad. The bread did.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of frozen slate blue, ash grey, tarnished brass, dirty snow white, and muted rust-orange for fire accents. Lighting mood is flat overcast winter — diffused, pale, shadowless, oppressive. Atmospheric quality progresses from industrial haze in early clips to heavy grey smoke and blowing snow in mid-to-late clips, with a final stillness of empty frozen streets. Recurring visual motifs: small dark bread loaves appearing in various states of diminishment across clips, and visible breath-fog rendered as flat white wisps against the cold grey air.
