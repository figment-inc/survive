# The Siege of Leningrad — 1941

**Slug**: `siege-of-leningrad-1941`
**Setting**: Leningrad, Soviet Union, September 1941 through early 1942 — the first and deadliest winter of the 872-day Nazi siege.
**Hook**: Three million people trapped with no food, no fuel, and no way out. You would not want to be in Leningrad, 1941.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of steel grey, cold slate blue, muted ochre, soot black, and bone white, with accents of dull amber fire and dried-blood burgundy. Lighting mood is flat overcast — an oppressive, low Soviet winter sky that never lifts. Atmospheric quality shifts from industrial haze in early clips to heavy woodsmoke and falling ash in catastrophe clips, then frozen stillness in the aftermath. Recurring visual motifs: a small, diminishing bread ration visible in multiple clips, and drifting grey ash or snow particles that fill the air in nearly every frame.
