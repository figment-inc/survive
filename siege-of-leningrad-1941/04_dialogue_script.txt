## Clip 01 (0:00-0:08) — The Hook
NARRATOR: You will survive the bombs. You will survive the shells. What nearly kills you is the bread. You would not want to be in Leningrad, 1941.

## Clip 02 (0:08-0:16) — Routine
NARRATOR: You are an engineer at the Kirov Plant, retooling tractor lines to build tanks. Grease blackens your knuckles to the wrist.

## Clip 03 (0:16-0:24) — False Safety
NARRATOR: The city holds three million people, a deep-water port, and more grain than Moscow. You feel the hum of a city that works.

## Clip 04 (0:24-0:32) — The Detail
NARRATOR: On September eighth, German artillery severs the last railway south. A single lake — Ladoga — remains between you and the rest of Russia.

## Clip 05 (0:32-0:40) — The Turn
NARRATOR: The warehouses burn that night. Melted sugar runs through the streets. The city's reserves drop to six days.

## Clip 06 (0:40-0:48) — Escalation
NARRATOR: Your bread ration falls to two hundred and fifty grams. Then one hundred and twenty-five. Sawdust fills the difference.

## Clip 07 (0:48-0:56) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Your gums bleed. Your hair falls. The wallpaper paste is gone. You boil your belt for broth.

## Clip 08 (0:56-1:04) — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Minus forty outside. The pipes burst. You drink snow from your windowsill and feel your teeth loosen.

## Clip 09 (1:04-1:12) — The Human Gesture
NARRATOR: A botanist at the seed bank starves to death at his desk — surrounded by tons of edible grain he refused to open.

## Clip 10 (1:12-1:20) — Aftermath
NARRATOR: The siege lasts eight hundred and seventy-two days. You walk past bodies stacked like cordwood each morning.

## Clip 11 (1:20-1:28) — The Number
NARRATOR: One million civilians dead. Most from hunger. Not a single bomb required.

## Clip 12 (1:28-1:36) — Callback
NARRATOR: The bread never stopped. One hundred and twenty-five grams of sawdust and suffering — just enough to make you survive it.
