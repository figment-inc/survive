## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Every building in Aleppo is heavy stone stacked on mud mortar. No one has felt an earthquake in generations, so no one built to survive one. You would not want to be in Aleppo, 1138.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: You live in a house built to stop swords, not tremors — heavy stone on mud mortar that shatters under any sideways force, walls packed so tight the whole block moves as one.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: At dawn, the ground lurches. You stumble into the street — your neighbors' walls are already cracking, shedding stone into the alley.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: You are one of 230,000 dead — in a fortress city whose walls were built for armies, not for the earth beneath them.
