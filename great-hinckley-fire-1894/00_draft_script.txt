## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Four hundred lumber mills have stripped Minnesota bare — and left the scraps piled across the forest floor. A drought turns it all to kindling. You would not want to be in Hinckley, 1894.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: Small fires have been smoldering for weeks. Nobody puts them out — burning slash is how mills clear land.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: The fires merge. Superheated air rises so fast it creates its own wind — a firestorm pulling in oxygen at hurricane speed.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: The wall of flame moves faster than a horse can run. You have minutes. The only escape is a train already backing out of town.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: The mills left the waste. The town left the fires burning. Hinckley disappeared in less than four hours.
