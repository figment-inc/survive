## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: Los Angeles builds a dam to steal water from a valley four hundred miles away. The chief engineer inspects a leak that morning and calls it safe. You would not want to be below the St. Francis Dam, 1928.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: William Mulholland designed the dam himself. No independent review. The canyon wall is ancient landslide debris — it dissolves when wet.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: Water seeps through the foundation for weeks. Mulholland sees muddy water pouring from the base and declares it harmless.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Three minutes before midnight, the entire dam gives way. A wall of water ten stories high hits you at eighteen miles per hour — in the dark.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: One man approved the site, the design, and the leaks. Over four hundred people were asleep downstream when his dam collapsed.
