## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 03):** "You watch muddy water pour from the base for weeks."
**Problem:** "You watch" is grammatically correct but experientially passive. The viewer is an observer, not a participant. This is the escalation clip — it should feel like the danger is closing on YOU, not like you're monitoring a situation.
**Fix:** "Muddy water seeps from the base for weeks. You report it — Mulholland inspects it and tells you it's harmless."

This makes "you" an agent who ACTS (reports), then gets dismissed. It also tightens the causal chain: you escalated, authority shut you down. Now the catastrophe is partly their fault AND you're trapped by their decision.

### Gut Punch — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "A wall of water ten stories high swallows your house before you open your eyes."
**Problem:** "Ten stories high" is impressive but abstract. "Swallows your house" is metaphorical and happens TO the house, not to your body. The strongest gut punch scripts put the disaster in the viewer's body — what do you FEEL? You're asleep. Then what? The line rushes past the human moment.
**Fix:** "Three minutes before midnight, the dam gives way. A wall of water ten stories high hits your town at thirty miles per hour. You never leave your bed."

"You never leave your bed" is colder, more final, and more physical than "before you open your eyes." It also removes the slightly awkward mixed image of swallowing a house and opening eyes in the same sentence.

---

This is a strong script. The causal chain is airtight, the Mulholland detail is an excellent Turn, and the closure pins accountability on a single human decision. The two notes above would push it from "good to ship" to "one of our best." But it clears the bar as-is.
