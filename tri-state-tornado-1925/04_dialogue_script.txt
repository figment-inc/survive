## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: A tornado touches down in Missouri and never lifts. For three and a half hours, it drags across three states. You would not want to be in its path, 1925.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: The U.S. Weather Bureau has banned the word "tornado" from forecasts — they think it would cause panic. You hear a roar and you have seconds to react.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: You survive the first hit, but the funnel doesn't dissipate — it accelerates to sixty miles an hour, faster than any car you own. It hits the next town nine minutes later.

## Clip 04 — Ending
NARRATOR: A single word banned from forecasts left 695 people with no warning — across a path that stretched 219 miles.
