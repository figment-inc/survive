## Clip 01 — Hook
NARRATOR: A tornado touches down in Missouri and never lifts. For three and a half hours, it drags across three states. You would not want to be in its path, 1925.

## Clip 02 — Setup
NARRATOR: There is no warning system. No radar. No sirens. You hear a roar and you have seconds.

## Clip 03 — Escalation
NARRATOR: The funnel is a mile wide and moving at seventy miles per hour — faster than any car you own.

## Clip 04 — Catastrophe
NARRATOR: Entire towns vanish. Murphysboro, Illinois loses forty blocks in minutes. You can't outrun it. You can't see it — rain hides it completely.

## Clip 05 — Ending
NARRATOR: People rebuilt on the same foundations. Nobody told them a tornado could stay on the ground for 219 miles — because nobody believed it could.
