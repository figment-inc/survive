## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "A tornado touches down in Missouri and never lifts. For three and a half hours, it drags across three states."
**Problem:** Two full sentences with zero "you." The tornado is the subject doing things. The viewer is watching a nature documentary for the first three seconds.
**Fix:** Keep the hook's factual punch but weave "you" in after the framing line: "A tornado touches down in Missouri and never lifts. For three and a half hours, it drags across three states. You would not want to be in its path, 1925." — The framing line rescues it, but "you" arriving only in sentence three is late. Consider: "A tornado touches down in Missouri and never lifts. You would not want to be in its path, 1925. For three and a half hours, it drags across three states." This also trims a beat from Clip 01 and moves duration to Clip 02.

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "Walls peel away. The floor lifts. Forty blocks of Murphysboro, gone in minutes."
**Problem:** "You" disappears entirely in the catastrophe clip — the moment it matters most. Walls and floors are the subjects. The viewer becomes a camera, not a victim.
**Fix:** "Your walls peel away. The floor lifts beneath you. Forty blocks of Murphysboro — gone in minutes."

### Gut Punch — 6/10

**Failing line (Clip 04):** "Walls peel away. The floor lifts. Forty blocks of Murphysboro, gone in minutes."
**Problem:** This is architecturally violent but not sensorily violent. The viewer sees buildings come apart but doesn't feel anything — no sound, no pressure, no helplessness. It reads like drone footage, not lived experience.
**Fix:** "Your walls tear loose. The floor lifts and you go with it. Forty blocks of Murphysboro — gone before anyone can scream." The addition of "you go with it" puts the viewer's body inside the event; "before anyone can scream" adds the temporal helplessness that makes it land.
