# The Sinking of the Mary Rose — 1545

**Slug**: `sinking-of-the-mary-rose-1545`
**Setting**: The Solent strait off Portsmouth, England, July 19, 1545, during the Battle of the Solent against a French invasion fleet.
**Hook**: The king watched from shore as his flagship rolled over and drowned 500 men in under a minute — trapped by their own anti-boarding nets.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of storm-grey sea, weathered oak brown, tarnished bronze, Tudor red, and sail-canvas cream, anchored by deep English Channel blue-green. Lighting is flat overcast — a heavy July sky casting diffused, shadowless light across choppy water. Atmospheric quality is salt-haze and gunsmoke hanging in flat grey-white layers across the strait. Recurring visual motifs: knotted rope netting criss-crossing frames, and the repeating geometry of open square gunports just above the waterline.
