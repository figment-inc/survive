# The Great Avalanche of Wellington — 1910

**Slug**: `great-avalanche-wellington-1910`
**Setting**: Wellington, Washington, a remote railroad stop high in the Cascade Mountains during a catastrophic blizzard, late February to early March 1910.
**Hook**: A blizzard traps your train on a mountain ledge for six days — then rain turns the entire mountainside into a weapon.

**Style Description**: Dominant palette of slate grey, blue-white snow, deep charcoal, iron black, and cold steel blue, with accents of dull rust-orange from train car paint and pale sickly yellow from kerosene lanterns rendered as flat color areas. Flat even lighting throughout — overcast, shadowless winter grey that flattens depth. Heavy atmospheric haze of blowing flat white snow particles filling the air in every frame, reducing visibility and creating claustrophobic compression. Recurring visual motifs: diagonal lines of falling snow or rain cutting across every frame, and the oppressive wall of the mountain slope looming above the train in nearly every composition.
