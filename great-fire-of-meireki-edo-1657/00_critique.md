## VERDICT: PUBLISH

This is one of the strongest scripts I've seen cross my desk. The causal chain is airtight, the escalation has two genuine turns (locked gates, then burning bridges), and the closure is a perfect revelation that recontextualizes the walls from protection to prison. The word count is exactly on target. The "January cold" detail does more sensory work than most scripts accomplish in an entire clip.

No axis scores below 7, so no line-level critique is required. A few minor observations for the writer's growth:

**Near-miss notes (not requiring fixes):**

- **Clip 01**: "A monk burns a cursed kimono" is evocative and memorable, but a viewer might briefly wonder why a monk is burning a kimono. The word "cursed" does enough work to justify it — the viewer fills in "ritual destruction" — but it's the one moment where the script asks the audience to infer rather than telling them. This is a feature, not a bug, at this word count.

- **Gut Punch (8, not 9)**: The script could push from 8 to 9 if Clip 04 had one more sensory beat — the crowd pressing you toward the water, the heat at your back — but at 90 words you have zero budget for it. The right call was made.

- **Closure**: "They kept a hundred thousand people in" — this is outstanding. The death toll is embedded inside the revelation rather than leading it. The irony lands and the scale lands simultaneously. This is the gold standard for how to end these scripts.

Ship it.
