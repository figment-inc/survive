## LINE-LEVEL CRITIQUE

### You-As-Subject — 7/10

**Failing line (Clip 01):** "Dresden has no air defenses. The Allies know this, so they send two waves — the first to start fires, the second to kill the firefighters."
**Problem:** The entire hook is third-person exposition. "Dresden" and "The Allies" are the subjects. "You" doesn't appear until the framing line, which is a tagline, not action. This is the clip where the viewer decides whether they're inside the story or watching from outside it.
**Fix:** "You're in a city with no air defenses. The Allies know this — so they plan two waves. The first to start fires. The second to kill the firefighters." — Now "you" is grounded in the city from the first sentence, and the Allies' plan is framed as something aimed *at you*.

**Failing line (Clip 05):** "Twenty-five thousand people burned in a city that couldn't shoot back — hit twice to make sure the rescuers died too."
**Problem:** "People" and "a city" are the subjects. The viewer has been yanked out of second-person into a news report. After four clips of being *inside* the firestorm, the ending should land on "you," not on an abstracted population.
**Fix:** "You're one of twenty-five thousand who burned in a city that couldn't shoot back — hit twice to make sure the rescuers died too." — Keeps the scale and the punch but anchors it in the viewer's body.

### Word Count — 7/10

**Problem:** 94 words. Four words over the 90-word ceiling. At 2.6 wps this runs ~36 seconds — still within Shorts range but pushing it.
**Fix:** Trim Clip 01's framing line "You would not want to be in Dresden, 1945" (10 words) and fold the location/date into a text overlay. This drops the script to 84 words and tightens the hook by ending on "the second to kill the firefighters" — which is a stronger cut-to-black moment anyway.
